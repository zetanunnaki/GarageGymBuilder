## 6-Month Durability Update

Six months of regular bodyweight training — pull-ups, dips, and knee raises 3 to 4 times per week. The frame has remained solid for our tester at 185 lbs. At that weight, there is minimal wobble during controlled movements. We re-torqued all bolts at the 3-month mark and found two that had loosened slightly, which is normal for welded tube construction under repeated load.

The arm and back padding has compressed about 15 to 20 percent from new. It is still comfortable for dip sets and knee raises, but users who train daily may want to replace the padding after 12 to 18 months. The pull-up bar grip is holding its texture without flaking.

The 250 lb weight limit is the biggest concern long-term. If you are over 200 lbs and plan to add a weighted vest, you are pushing the rated capacity. Stick to strict movements — kipping pull-ups at higher body weights will stress the frame beyond its design.

## Compared to Alternatives

**Stamina 1690 vs Sportsroyals Power Tower ($200-250):** The Sportsroyals has a higher weight capacity (400 lbs), thicker tubing, and an adjustable backrest. It is a meaningful upgrade for heavier users or anyone who wants to do weighted dips and pull-ups. If you are under 200 lbs and training bodyweight only, the Stamina saves you $80 and does the job.

**Stamina 1690 vs Doorway Pull-Up Bar ($25-35):** A doorway pull-up bar handles pull-ups and chin-ups at a fraction of the cost, but gives you nothing for dips, knee raises, or push-up variations. The power tower is worth the investment if you want a complete bodyweight station — especially for dips, which are hard to replicate with a doorway bar.
