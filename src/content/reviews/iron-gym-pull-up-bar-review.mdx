## Why a Doorway Bar Makes Sense

For most apartment dwellers, college students, and budget-conscious lifters, a doorway pull-up bar is the only practical way to do pull-ups at home. You don't need:

- A power rack ($330+)
- A wall-mounted bar (needs drilling)
- A free-standing station ($200+ and 20 sq ft)

You just need a doorframe and $30. The Iron Gym hooks over your existing doorframe, no installation required.

## Who Should Buy It

**Buy it if:**
- You live in an apartment or rental

- You're new to pull-ups
- You need a portable solution for travel
- You want the cheapest possible pull-up bar

**Skip it if:**
- You have a power rack already
- You weigh over 250 lbs
- You want to do kipping pull-ups or muscle-ups
- Your doorways are non-standard

## Doorframe Damage Risk

The biggest complaint about doorway bars is doorframe damage. The Iron Gym uses leverage to hold itself in place — outward pressure on both sides of the frame. With heavy use, this can:

- Compress trim over time
- Crack older trim
- Leave marks on the painted surface

To minimize damage:
- Use foam padding under the contact points
- Don't leave the bar installed permanently
- Avoid kipping or jerking movements
- Choose a doorframe with solid wood trim, not MDF
