## 6-Month Durability Update

After six months of 4 to 5 training sessions per week, our Harbinger gloves are showing honest wear. The leather palm has darkened and softened — it actually grips better now than when new. The stitching along the finger seams is holding up with no loose threads. The wrist wrap velcro still bites firmly.

The mesh backing has stretched slightly, giving the gloves a more relaxed fit compared to day one. This is normal for leather and mesh construction. If you are between sizes, go with the smaller option since they will break in. The pull tab on the back of each finger is still attached and functional, which is where most cheap gloves fail first.

## Compared to Alternatives

**Harbinger Pro vs Mechanix Wear Work Gloves:** Some lifters prefer Mechanix M-Pact gloves ($25-30) for gym use. They offer better dexterity and a more fitted feel, but they lack the integrated wrist wrap and the palm padding is thinner. For pure weightlifting, the Harbinger is purpose-built. For a mixed-use glove (gym plus yard work), Mechanix might be more versatile.

**Harbinger Pro vs Going Bare-Handed with Chalk:** Many experienced lifters skip gloves entirely and use chalk for grip. This gives better bar feel and stronger grip development over time. Gloves are best for lifters who want callus prevention, those with skin conditions, or anyone training with equipment that tears up hands (like aggressive pull-up bar knurling).
