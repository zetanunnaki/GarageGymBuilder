## 6-Month Durability Update

Six months of daily use on our Olympic barbell — squats, bench, deadlifts, overhead press. The lever mechanism still snaps into place with a satisfying click, and the clamping force has not diminished. The nylon liner inside the collar shows no wear or cracking. The anodized aluminum finish has a few minor scuffs from being dropped on concrete, but no structural damage.

We tested these on three different barbells — a Rogue Ohio Bar, a CAP barbell, and a budget Amazon bar. They fit all three securely, though the budget bar's slightly thinner sleeves allowed a tiny amount of play. On the Rogue and CAP bars, the fit was rock-solid with zero plate movement during heavy lifts.

## Compared to Alternatives

**Synergee Aluminum vs Spring Clips:** Spring clips are the $5 collars that come with most barbell sets. They work, but they slip under heavy loads, are harder to put on and take off, and can scratch bar sleeves. The Synergee collars are a genuine quality-of-life upgrade — faster to use, more secure, and gentler on your equipment. This is one of the cheapest upgrades that makes every training session better.

**Synergee Aluminum vs Rogue OSO Collars:** The Rogue OSO ($40+) is the premium option with tighter tolerances and a heavier-duty lever mechanism. For most home gym lifters, the Synergee holds plates just as securely at nearly half the price. The Rogue is worth it if you are loading 400+ lbs and need absolute zero plate movement, or if you want the Rogue aesthetic.
