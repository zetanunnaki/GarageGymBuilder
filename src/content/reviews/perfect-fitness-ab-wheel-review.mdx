## Why the Ab Wheel Beats Crunches

The science is clear:

1. **Trains the entire core** — abs, obliques, lower back, hip flexors all engaged
2. **Anti-extension** — the most functional core movement (resisting arching)
3. **Progressive overload** — extend further as you get stronger
4. **No spinal flexion** — safer for backs than crunches

## Beginner Progression

The ab wheel looks easy. It's not. Start here:

### Week 1-2: Wall Rollouts
Stand 2 feet from a wall, roll the wheel up the wall, let your body lean forward. Build core strength before going to the floor.

### Week 3-4: Knee Rollouts (Half Range)
Knees on a pad, roll forward until you're at 45 degrees. Roll back. Repeat.

### Week 5-8: Knee Rollouts (Full Range)
Roll forward until your chest is just above the floor. The hardest part is the return — most beginners can't pull themselves back.

### Month 3+: Standing Rollouts
The advanced version. Standing on your feet, roll out to a full plank position, then back. Brutal.

Don't skip phases. Most beginners try standing rollouts day one and pull a muscle.

## The Spring Advantage

Most ab wheels have no return assistance. You either generate enough core force to pull yourself back, or you collapse and have to push back up with your hands.

The Perfect Fitness Ab Carver has a carbon steel spring inside that adds about 10-20% return assistance. This is crucial for beginners — you can extend further than your raw strength allows, then the spring helps you return.

For advanced users, the spring becomes a slight handicap. But by then you've already built the strength to use a basic wheel anyway.

## Best Workouts

### Beginner (3 days/week)
- 3 sets of 5 knee rollouts, half range
- Progress to full range over 4-6 weeks

### Intermediate (3 days/week)
- 4 sets of 8-12 knee rollouts, full range
- Add a 5-second pause at the bottom of the last rep

### Advanced (3 days/week)
- 5 sets of 5 standing rollouts
- Mix in side rollouts (rolling at 45 degree angles for obliques)
