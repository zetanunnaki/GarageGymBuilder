## Assembly & First Impressions

Assembly takes about 15 minutes with minimal tools. The bench comes mostly pre-assembled — you're basically just attaching the legs. It folds flat when not in use, which is a huge win for small garage gyms.

## Build Quality

The steel frame is heavier than expected at this price point. The welds aren't beautiful, but they're functional. The vinyl pad is comfortable and has held up well — no tears or compression after 6 months of regular use.

## The Gap Problem

Let's address the elephant in the room: at incline angles above 45 degrees, there's a noticeable gap between the seat pad and backrest. This can feel uncomfortable during heavy incline presses. It's not dangerous, but it's the main trade-off at this price point.

**The fix:** Use it primarily at flat and low incline angles where the gap is minimal. For steep incline work, a fixed-position bench would be better.

## FLYBIRD vs REP FB-5000

The REP FB-5000 ($349) is wider, has no gap, and supports 1,000 lbs. But it also costs 2.5x more and doesn't fold. For most home gym lifters working with moderate weight, the FLYBIRD delivers 80% of the experience at 40% of the price.

## Who Should Buy This?

The FLYBIRD is perfect for:
- Beginners building their first home gym
- Lifters on a tight budget
- Anyone who needs a foldable bench for space reasons
- Supplementary bench for a gym that already has a flat bench

Skip it if you're benching 300+ lbs regularly or need a competition-width pad.

## 6-Month Durability Update

At the six-month mark, the FLYBIRD is showing expected wear for the price. The vinyl pad has a few minor scuff marks but no tears or significant compression. The adjustment ladder has actually loosened up and now clicks into position more smoothly than it did when new — the initial stiffness was a break-in issue, not a design flaw.

We have had zero issues with the locking mechanism or the folding hinge. One user on our team benches 225 lbs regularly on this bench and reports no wobble or flex at the flat position. At incline, there is a slight flex under heavy load, but nothing that feels unsafe. The rubber feet have stayed in place, and we have not needed to re-tighten any bolts.
