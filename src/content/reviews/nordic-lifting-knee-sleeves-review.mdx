## Why 7mm is the Standard

Knee sleeves come in three thicknesses:

- **5mm:** Warmth + mild compression. Good for general fitness.
- **7mm:** Powerlifting standard. Noticeable rebound out of the squat.
- **9mm:** Very stiff, hard to put on, used by elite meet lifters.

For 95% of lifters, 7mm is the right choice. Enough support for heavy squats without being so stiff you can't walk normally.

## Sizing — Read This Before Buying

This is the #1 complaint in Amazon reviews. The Nordic Lifting size chart is technically accurate but cautious. Most users report needing one size larger than the chart suggests.

**My advice:** Measure around the middle of your knee cap with slight flexion, then order ONE SIZE UP from the chart. If you're between sizes, always round up. Tight sleeves are warmer but brutal to put on — and nobody wants to fight their equipment for 10 minutes before every session.

## When to Wear Them

Knee sleeves aren't always appropriate. Here's the breakdown:

- **Back squat 80%+ 1RM:** Yes, always
- **Front squat heavy:** Yes
- **Bodyweight squats:** No, overkill
- **Leg press:** Optional
- **Deadlifts:** No (not needed, and illegal in most federations)
- **Olympic lifts:** Yes, especially cleans and jerks

## How Long Do They Last?

With proper care (no machine drying, no bleach, rinse in cold water after sweaty sessions), expect 3-5 years. The neoprene will eventually lose some of its snap, but the sleeves remain functional for much longer than they feel new.

## Who Should Buy Them

**Buy them if:**
- You back squat 225+ lbs regularly
- You have minor knee discomfort during heavy squats
- You train in a cold garage gym
- You want budget-friendly powerlifting gear
- You're new to knee sleeves and don't want to spend $80+

**Skip them if:**
- You compete in IPF powerlifting (need Rehband or SBD)
