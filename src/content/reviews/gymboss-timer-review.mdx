## 3-Month Durability Update

Three months of regular use across HIIT sessions, rest-pause training, and EMOM workouts. The timer has been dropped on concrete twice and survived both falls without any damage. The belt clip is secure and has not loosened. Battery life is excellent — we are still on the original battery after three months of 4 to 5 uses per week.

The buttons remain clicky and responsive. Programming intervals becomes second nature after the first week — the learning curve is the only real hurdle with this device. Once you have your Tabata or EMOM intervals saved, it is literally clip-and-go.

## Compared to Alternatives

**Gymboss vs Phone Timer App:** Free timer apps work, but they require your phone to be unlocked and visible. During heavy barbell work, nobody wants to touch their phone with chalky hands. The Gymboss clips to your shorts and beeps or vibrates — no screen tapping required. It also removes the distraction of having your phone nearby during training.

**Gymboss vs Large LED Gym Timer ($80-150):** Wall-mounted gym timers are great if you have a dedicated space and want visibility from across the room.
