## Installation Tips

Mounting these mirrors requires some planning. We used J-channel mirror clips (sold separately, about $10 on Amazon) and toggle bolts into drywall. The process took about 45 minutes per mirror with two people. If your garage has exposed studs or concrete block, use appropriate anchors — the mirror weighs around 20 lbs and needs secure mounting.

Position the mirror so you can see your full body during squats and deadlifts. For most people, the bottom edge should be 12 to 18 inches from the floor. Mounting 2 to 3 of these side by side covers a standard lifting area.

## Compared to Alternatives

**Fab Glass vs Glacier Bay Door Mirror ($10):** Cheap door mirrors from hardware stores are thin, warp your reflection, and shatter into dangerous shards. The Fab Glass tempered panel gives you a true, distortion-free reflection that breaks into small safe pieces if somehow impacted. For a training environment, tempered glass is non-negotiable.

**Fab Glass vs Acrylic Gym Mirrors ($60-80):** Acrylic mirrors are lighter and genuinely shatterproof, but they scratch easily and produce a slightly wavy reflection. If you have kids or throw implements near the mirror, acrylic is safer. For a standard lifting setup, real tempered glass gives a better image quality.
