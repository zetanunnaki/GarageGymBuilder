## Why a Trap Bar Belongs in Every Home Gym

Straight-bar deadlifts are the king of strength exercises, but they're brutal on your lower back if your form slips. The trap bar (also called hex bar) puts the load *around* you instead of in front of you. Result:

- More upright torso position
- Less shear force on the spine
- Easier to learn for beginners
- Carries over to athletic movements (jumps, sprints)

For most home gym owners, a trap bar is the second-most-important bar you'll own (after a straight Olympic bar).

## High Handle vs Low Handle

The dual-handle design is the trap bar's secret weapon:

**Low handles (deadlift position):**
- Mimics conventional deadlift range of motion
- Best for max strength
- Harder for tall lifters or those with mobility issues

**High handles (raised position):**
- ~4 inches shorter range of motion
- Easier on hips and lower back
- Best for beginners and high-rep work

Most users start with high handles and progress to low handles as mobility improves.
