## 6-Month Durability Update

Six months of consistent grip training — 3 sessions per week, working through the Trainer (100 lbs), Point Five (120 lbs), and No. 1 (140 lbs) grippers. The spring tension has not changed at all. These are built from GR8 spring steel that IronMind heat-treats specifically to maintain consistent resistance over years of use. The aluminum handles show finger marks but no structural wear.

The knurling on the handles provides just enough grip to hold the gripper securely without tearing up your skin. Some users wrap athletic tape around the handles for extra comfort on high-rep sets, but we found the stock handles fine for sets under 15 reps.

## Compared to Alternatives

**IronMind CoC vs Heavy Grips:** Heavy Grips are cheaper (about $15 each) and come in similar resistance levels. However, their quality control is less consistent — two grippers rated at the same resistance can feel noticeably different. IronMind's precision manufacturing means every CoC gripper of the same model feels identical, which matters for tracking progress.

**IronMind CoC vs Adjustable Hand Grippers ($10-15):** Adjustable grippers from Amazon let you dial in any resistance from about 20 to 130 lbs. They are convenient for warm-ups, but the mechanism feels cheap and often breaks within a few months. The CoC is a buy-once, use-forever tool. Serious grip training demands fixed-resistance grippers.
