## 6-Month Durability Update

Six months in, the chrome plating on our CAP EZ curl bar is holding up without any flaking, chipping, or rust spots. We store it in an unheated garage that fluctuates between 30 and 90 degrees, and it has shown no corrosion. The bushing sleeves still rotate smoothly — nothing compared to needle bearings, but perfectly adequate for curls, skull crushers, and upright rows.

The knurling remains the same light texture as day one. It was never aggressive to begin with, which is actually fine for a curl bar since you are not doing heavy pulls. On humid days we use liquid chalk, but that is more of a preference than a necessity.

## Compared to Alternatives

**CAP EZ Curl Bar vs XMark Crowbar:** The XMark Crowbar ($90+) has more aggressive knurling, needle bearings for spin, and a higher weight capacity. If you are doing heavy preacher curls over 150 lbs or want a bar that rotates smoothly, the XMark is the upgrade. For most home gym lifters curling under 100 lbs, the CAP does everything you need at half the price.

**CAP EZ Curl Bar vs Titan EZ Curl Bar:** Nearly identical specifications and price. The Titan version has slightly longer sleeves, which can matter if you are loading a lot of plates. Otherwise, buy whichever is in stock and shipping faster.
