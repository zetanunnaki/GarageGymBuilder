## What Percussive Therapy Actually Does

Before we review the device, let us be clear about what it does and does not do. A massage gun delivers rapid, repetitive pressure pulses into muscle tissue. The evidence-supported benefits are:

1. **Increased blood flow** to the treated area, which can accelerate recovery
2. **Reduced perceived muscle soreness** (DOMS) when used within 24 hours after training
3. **Temporary increase in range of motion** — useful as a warm-up tool
4. **Pain gate mechanism** — the vibration signals override pain signals, providing short-term relief

What it does not do: fix injuries, replace stretching and mobility work, or substitute for sleep and nutrition in recovery. It is a supplement to a recovery protocol, not a replacement.

## The Device

The Theragun Mini 2nd Gen fits in your palm. It measures roughly 5.5 x 4 x 2.5 inches and weighs 1.43 lbs — lighter than most water bottles. The body is a matte-finish polymer that grips well with sweaty hands.

### Three Speed Settings

- **Speed 1 (1,750 PPM):** Low percussion rate. This is the warm-up and cool-down setting, ideal for sensitive areas like the neck, forearms, and shins. The pressure is gentle enough for daily use without irritation.
- **Speed 2 (2,100 PPM):** Medium percussion rate. The workhorse setting for post-workout quad, hamstring, and shoulder recovery. Most of our use fell on this setting.
- **Speed 3 (2,400 PPM):** High percussion rate. This delivers aggressive therapy for deep knots, stubborn trigger points, and dense muscle groups like the glutes and upper back. Firm pressure at this speed can be uncomfortable on sore muscles — in a good way.

### The Motor

The QX35 motor delivers a 12mm amplitude — the depth the attachment head travels into your muscle. This is where the Theragun Mini separates from budget guns. Most $30-50 massage guns deliver 6-8mm amplitude. The difference is significant: 12mm reaches deeper muscle fibers, particularly in thick muscle groups like the quads, glutes, and lats. A shallow 6mm percussion feels like a surface vibration. The 12mm Mini feels like it is actually reaching the tight tissue.

The motor also maintains speed under pressure. When you press the Mini hard into a dense muscle, it does not stall or slow down noticeably. Budget guns lose RPM under pressure, which reduces their effectiveness on the areas that need the most work.

### Battery Life

Therabody rates the Mini at 150 minutes of battery life. In our testing, we averaged approximately 120-130 minutes of actual use before the battery indicator turned red. We used the device for 5-10 minutes per session, which translates to roughly 15-20 sessions per charge. Charging takes about 80 minutes via the included USB-C cable.

For a device you use daily, the battery life is excellent. We charged it roughly once every two weeks with daily post-workout use.

### Noise Levels

At Speed 1, the Mini is quiet enough to use during a phone call. At Speed 2, it produces a low hum that is easily masked by background music. At Speed 3, it is noticeable but not disruptive — significantly quieter than a hair dryer and far quieter than first-generation massage guns.

If you train early morning and share a bedroom or apartment, the Mini at Speed 1-2 will not wake anyone in an adjacent room.

## How to Use the Theragun Mini for Recovery

Here is a post-workout protocol we developed over four months:

### Post-Strength Training (5-7 minutes total)
1. **Quads:** 60 seconds per leg, Speed 2, slow sweeping motions from hip to knee
2. **Hamstrings:** 60 seconds per leg, Speed 2, focus on the belly of the muscle
3. **Upper back/traps:** 60 seconds, Speed 2, avoid the spine — work the muscle tissue on either side
4. **Shoulders (front and rear delts):** 30 seconds per side, Speed 1
5. **Forearms:** 30 seconds per arm, Speed 1 — especially after heavy gripping work

### Pre-Workout Warm-Up (2-3 minutes)
1. Target the primary muscle groups for that session at Speed 1
2. 30-60 seconds per muscle group
3. Follow with dynamic stretching
4. This increases blood flow and range of motion before loading

### Rest Day Recovery (10 minutes)
1. Full body sweep at Speed 1-2
2. Extra time on sore or tight areas
3. 60-90 seconds per muscle group
4. Combine with gentle stretching for maximum effect

## Theragun Mini vs Budget Massage Guns ($30-50)

The budget guns on Amazon deliver vibration. The Theragun Mini delivers percussion. The difference matters:

**Amplitude:** Budget guns typically offer 6-8mm amplitude. The Mini delivers 12mm. Deeper penetration reaches tight tissue that surface vibration cannot.

**Stall force:** Budget guns slow down or stall when you apply pressure. The Mini maintains speed under force. On dense muscle groups like the glutes and upper back, budget guns essentially become expensive vibrators — the percussion disappears under pressure.

**Build quality:** Budget guns use cheap plastic housings and generic brushless motors. They work for a few months, then develop rattling, battery issues, or motor failure. The Mini uses Therabody's QX35 motor and premium materials designed for years of daily use.

**Is the $100+ premium worth it?** For casual users who want occasional light recovery, a budget gun is fine. For athletes who train hard 4-6 days a week and rely on percussion therapy as part of a recovery protocol, the Mini's deeper amplitude and stall resistance make a meaningful difference.

## Theragun Mini vs Theragun PRO ($399)

The PRO delivers 16mm amplitude (vs 12mm), has a rotating arm for reaching your own back, includes 5 attachment heads, and offers 5 speed settings. It is a significantly better device.

But the PRO costs $399, weighs 2.2 lbs, and is bulky. The Mini at $149 delivers approximately 75% of the PRO's effectiveness in a package that weighs 65% less and costs 63% less. For most home gym athletes who are not professional or collegiate athletes, the Mini is the sweet spot of effectiveness and value.

## 4-Month Durability Report

After four months of daily use (averaging 5-7 minutes per session):

- **Motor:** No change in power or speed. Still maintains percussion under pressure.
- **Battery:** Still holds approximately 120 minutes of use per charge. No noticeable degradation.
- **Attachment head:** Shows minor wear marks on the surface foam. Still functional.
- **Body:** Minor cosmetic scuffs from being tossed in a gym bag. No cracks or structural issues.

## Who Should Buy the Theragun Mini

**Buy it if:**
- You train 4+ days per week and deal with regular muscle soreness
- You want a portable recovery tool that fits in a gym bag or desk drawer
- You value percussion depth over price — the 12mm amplitude matters
- You want a device that will last years with daily use
- You do not need to reach your own mid/lower back (or have a partner/wall technique)

**Skip it if:**
- Budget is tight — a $40 Amazon gun provides basic vibration therapy
- You need deep tissue work on your own back — the PRO's rotating arm is worth the upgrade
- You are looking for a full-body recovery solution — combine with foam rolling and stretching
- You rarely train hard enough to be sore — you will not use it enough to justify $149
