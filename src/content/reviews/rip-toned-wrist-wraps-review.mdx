## Wrist Wraps vs Wrist Straps

Important distinction — beginners confuse these:

- **Wrist WRAPS** (this product): Support the wrist joint during pressing. Think bench, overhead press.
- **Wrist STRAPS**: Improve grip on the bar during pulling. Think deadlift, rows.

These are different tools for different lifts. Wraps go on your wrist, straps connect your wrist to the bar.

## When to Wear Wrist Wraps

Wrist wraps aren't always appropriate. Use them for:

- **Heavy bench press (80%+ 1RM)** — yes
- **Heavy overhead press** — yes
- **Push press and jerks** — yes
- **Dumbbell chest press with heavy weights** — yes
- **Moderate presses (60-70%)** — optional
- **Warm-ups and light sets** — no
- **Bodyweight dips and push-ups** — no

Don't wear them for every set. Your wrists need to develop some of their own strength, not rely entirely on external support.

## How to Wrap Correctly

1. **Start with thumb loop around thumb** — prevents slipping
2. **Wrap below the wrist joint first** — stabilize the base
3. **Cross over the wrist joint twice** — main support zone
4. **Finish above the joint** — locks in rotation
5. **Velcro should be tight but not cut circulation** — fingers should still move

Loose wraps are useless. Tight wraps restrict blood flow. Find the middle ground — firm but not painful.
