## Best Ring Exercises

Start basic, progress gradually:

### Beginner
1. **Ring rows** — easier than pull-ups, full back development
2. **Ring push-ups** — instability builds shoulder stability
3. **Ring planks** — anti-gravity core training

### Intermediate
4. **Ring dips** — more challenging than parallel bar dips
5. **Ring chin-ups** — easier on the wrists than fixed bars
6. **Ring rows (feet elevated)** — progression toward inverted rows

### Advanced
7. **Muscle-ups** — the holy grail of bodyweight training
8. **Front lever** — straight-body horizontal hold
9. **Iron cross** — elite strength move

You'll never run out of progressions on rings.

## Setup Tips

The rings need a sturdy overhead anchor. Options:
- **Power rack pull-up bar** (best — already in your gym)
- **Tree branch** (outdoor — verify branch can hold your bodyweight)
- **Ceiling joist hook** (permanent install for garage gyms)
- **Pull-up bar** (works but adds wear to spring-loaded doorway bars — avoid)

Set the rings at chest height for ring rows, shoulder height for dips, and 6+ feet up for pull-ups and muscle-ups.

## Who Should Buy Them

**Buy them if:**
- You have a power rack or sturdy anchor point
- You want to progress beyond pull-ups
- You do CrossFit or calisthenics
- You hate boring traditional pull-ups
- You want to learn muscle-ups eventually

**Skip them if:**
- You don't have a sturdy overhead anchor
- You only train traditional weightlifting
- You weigh under 250 lbs and find pull-ups easy already (rings won't add much)
