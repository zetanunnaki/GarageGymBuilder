## What's in the Box

- 1x 7-foot Olympic barbell (45 lbs, ~28mm shaft)
- 2x 45 lb cast iron plates
- 2x 35 lb cast iron plates
- 2x 25 lb cast iron plates
- 2x 10 lb cast iron plates
- 2x 5 lb cast iron plates

Total weight: 300 lbs (255 lbs in plates + 45 lb bar)

## The Barbell

Let's be honest: the bar is the weak link. It's a basic 28mm chrome-plated bar with bushing sleeves. The knurling is mild, the whip is nonexistent, and the spin is sluggish.

But here's the thing: for squats, bench, deadlifts, overhead press, and rows — it works fine. You don't need a $300 barbell to get strong. You need a barbell, period.

## 9-Month Durability Report

After 9 months of 4-day-per-week training:
- The bar has developed minor chrome wear in the knurl area (cosmetic only)
- No permanent bend despite regularly loading 315+ lbs
- Sleeves still spin, though slower than day one
- Plates show normal wear — paint chips but no cracking

## When Should You Upgrade the Bar?

Upgrade when any of these apply:
- You're deadlifting 400+ lbs and need better grip
- You want to do cleans, snatches, or other Olympic lifts
- You're benching 300+ and want the confidence of a better bar
- The sleeves stop spinning altogether

Keep the plates forever. Cast iron doesn't expire.

## Final Verdict

**Rating: 3.8/5** — The CAP 300 lb set is the right first purchase for anyone building a home gym on a budget. The bar is mediocre but functional, and the plates will serve you for years. Buy this, train hard, and upgrade the bar when your strength demands it.
