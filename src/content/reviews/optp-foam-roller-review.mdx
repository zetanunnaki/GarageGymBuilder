## 6-Month Durability Update

Six months of daily rolling — quads, IT band, thoracic spine, and calves after every training session. The closed-cell foam has held its density and shape remarkably well. Unlike cheap open-cell rollers that flatten within a few months, the OPTP still feels the same as day one. No dents, no soft spots, no peeling surface material.

We have stored it standing upright in the corner of the gym and occasionally left it in a hot garage (100+ degrees). No warping or melting. The plain cylindrical design means there are no ridges or bumps to break off — it is as simple and durable as a foam roller can be.

## Compared to Alternatives

**OPTP PRO-ROLLER vs TriggerPoint GRID:** The TriggerPoint GRID ($35) has a textured surface that provides more targeted pressure on knots and trigger points. It is also hollow, making it lighter and travel-friendly. The OPTP is solid foam, which means it provides more even pressure across large muscle groups. For general recovery rolling, the OPTP is gentler and more versatile. For deep tissue work on specific knots, the GRID is better.

**OPTP PRO-ROLLER vs High-Density Foam Rollers:** If you find the OPTP too soft, consider a high-density or EPP roller. These are significantly firmer and better for experienced users with dense muscle tissue. However, the standard density OPTP is what most physical therapists recommend for daily maintenance rolling because it is effective without being painful.
