## 6-Month Durability Update

Six months of twice-weekly arm sessions, primarily EZ bar curls and dumbbell concentration curls. The 11-gauge steel frame is as solid as day one — no flex, no wobble, no loose bolts. The DuraFoam arm pad shows sweat stains but no tears or significant compression. We wipe it down after each session with a damp cloth, which keeps it in good condition.

The seat height adjustment has been reliable, though the pop-pin mechanism requires a firm pull. Multiple users in our gym (ranging from 5'4" to 6'2") all found a comfortable setting. The vinyl on the seat pad is holding up well, though it is smoother than the arm pad and can get slippery with sweat — placing a small towel on it solves this.

## Compared to Alternatives

**XMark Preacher Curl vs Body-Solid Preacher Curl Bench:** The Body-Solid GPCB329 ($180+) has a wider arm pad and heavier frame, but costs about $40 more. For most home gym lifters, the XMark provides the same isolation and training effect at a better price. The Body-Solid is worth the upgrade only if you are doing heavy preacher curls over 120 lbs.

**XMark Preacher Curl vs Preacher Curl Attachment for Power Rack:** Some power rack brands offer bolt-on preacher curl attachments for $50-80. These save space since they integrate with your existing rack, but they are often less comfortable and harder to adjust. The XMark standalone bench provides a better curl experience and can be placed anywhere in your gym.
