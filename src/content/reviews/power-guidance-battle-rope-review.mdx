## Anchoring the Rope

The rope needs to loop around something fixed. Options:

### 1. Outdoor Tree
Best option. Use the included anchor strap around a 6+ inch diameter tree. Position the rope so you're standing 15 feet away (half the rope length).

### 2. Power Rack Base
If your rack is bolted to concrete, you can loop the rope around a base upright. Check for rope wear on metal edges.

### 3. Post or Pole
Any fixed metal post works. Lamp posts, fence posts, pergola supports.

### 4. Heavy Kettlebell or Barbell
Not ideal — the rope can pull it. Weight the anchor with 100+ lbs.

## Battle Rope Workouts

Three brutal workouts to try:

### 1. The 30-30 (10 rounds)
- 30 seconds all-out rope waves
- 30 seconds rest
- Repeat 10 times
- Total: 10 minutes, pure suffering

### 2. Tabata Rope (4 rounds)
- 20 seconds waves
- 10 seconds rest
- Repeat 8 times (1 round)
- Rest 1 minute
- Repeat for 4 total rounds

### 3. Rope + Burpees (AMRAP 15)
- 20 double waves
- 10 burpees
- Continue as many rounds as possible in 15 minutes

Any of these will leave you destroyed. Battle ropes don't joke.

## Technique Tips

1. **Hips and legs matter** — don't just use your arms. Drive waves from your legs up
2. **Keep a soft knee bend** — don't lock out
3. **Stand far enough back** — 15 ft for a 30 ft rope
4. **Vary the movement** — alternating waves, double waves, slams, circles
5. **Don't grip too hard** — light grip keeps your forearms fresh
