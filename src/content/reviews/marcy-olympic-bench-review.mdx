## The 300 lb Limit

The biggest limitation of the MD-857 is the 300 lb maximum on the bar. For most beginners, this is plenty — a 300 lb bench press is an intermediate strength level. But once you're pressing 300+ lbs, you'll need to upgrade to a dedicated power rack.

**Timeline for most lifters:**
- **0-6 months:** MD-857 handles everything
- **6-24 months:** Still works, approaching limits
- **2+ years:** Upgrade to a dedicated power rack

## Marcy MD-857 vs FLYBIRD Bench + Squat Stand

If you're choosing between the Marcy all-in-one or separate equipment:

**Marcy MD-857 ($240):** Complete station, includes rack catches, leg developer, 300 lb limit
**FLYBIRD ($110) + Squat Stand ($120):** More flexible, higher weight limits, more floor space needed

The Marcy is better for beginners who want simplicity. The separate setup is better for lifters planning to progress to heavier weights.

## Who Should Buy It?

**Buy the Marcy MD-857 if:**
- You're a home gym beginner
- You're on a tight budget (~$240 for complete station)
- Your heaviest lifts stay under 300 lbs

**Skip it if:**
- You're already benching 300+ lbs
