## 3-Month Durability Update

After three months of daily training on these mats — including barbell work, dumbbell drops, and bodyweight circuits — they are holding up well. The interlocking seams have separated in one spot under our power rack where the uprights sit, but everywhere else the fit remains tight. The foam has compressed slightly under the heaviest traffic areas (directly in front of the rack), but not enough to affect cushioning or noise reduction.

The chemical smell disappeared completely within the first week. We wiped them down with a damp cloth and mild soap once a week, and the surface shows no discoloration or breakdown. For $26, these mats are performing well above their price point.

## Compared to Alternatives

**BalanceFrom vs Horse Stall Mats:** Horse stall mats (about $45 for a 4x6 mat) are denser, heavier, and far more durable for deadlifts and heavy drops. But they weigh 100 lbs each, smell worse initially, and cannot be easily cut or reconfigured. The BalanceFrom mats are the better choice for general training areas; stall mats are better directly under your deadlift platform.

**BalanceFrom vs Prosource Puzzle Mats:** Very similar products at similar prices. The Prosource mats are marginally thicker (3/4 inch vs 1/2 inch) but cover less area per pack. For most home gym setups, the BalanceFrom pack gives you better total coverage per dollar.
