## Magnetic vs Air Resistance

Before buying any rower, understand the difference:

**Magnetic resistance (this rower):**
- Whisper quiet (won't wake the family)
- Smooth, consistent feel
- Manual resistance adjustment (8 levels)
- Lower max resistance ceiling

**Air resistance (Concept2):**
- Loud — sounds like a windstorm
- Resistance increases with effort (no settings)
- Unlimited resistance ceiling
- Industry standard for competition rowing

For 90% of home gym users training for general fitness, magnetic is the better choice. Quiet, smooth, and plenty of resistance for hard workouts. Save $700 and buy the Sunny.

## Setup and Storage

The SF-RW5515 ships in a box you can carry yourself (~57 lbs). Assembly takes 30-45 minutes with the included tools. The unique selling point: it folds vertically for storage. Unfolded it's 82" long, but folded it stands ~52" tall and takes up about 25" x 19" of floor space.

This is a major win for small spaces. Most rowers either don't fold at all or fold awkwardly. The Sunny actually folds in a way that's useful.

## Why Rowing Beats Most Cardio

If you only buy one cardio machine, make it a rower. Here's why:

- **Full-body workout** — legs, back, arms, core all engaged
- **Low impact** — no joint pounding (unlike running)
- **High calorie burn** — ~600 cal/hr at moderate effort
- **Builds strength endurance** — actually carries over to lifting
- **Fits any program** — warm-up, finisher, steady-state cardio, intervals

A 20-minute rowing session is more effective than a 30-minute treadmill walk for most people.

## Programming Suggestions

### For Conditioning
- 5 min easy warm-up
- 10 x 250m intervals at 80% effort, 1 min rest between
- 5 min cool down

### For Steady-State Cardio
- 30-45 min at moderate pace
- Heart rate ~70% of max
- 2-3x per week as zone-2 training

### For Strength Conditioning
- 5 rounds: 500m row + 10 push-ups + 10 air squats
- Rest as needed between rounds
- Total time ~20 minutes
