## 6-Month Durability Update

After six months of regular use on barbells, dumbbells, and pull-up bars, our Fat Gripz show no signs of tearing, cracking, or losing their grip. The rubber compound is impressively durable — we have left them clamped on a pull-up bar for weeks at a time with no deformation. The inner surface still grips bars tightly without slipping during heavy sets.

The only wear we have noticed is a slight smoothing of the outer texture where our thumbs wrap around. This has not affected grip performance in any meaningful way. These feel like they will last for years.

## Compared to Alternatives

**Fat Gripz Original vs Fat Gripz Extreme:** The Extreme version increases diameter to 2.75 inches (vs 2.25 for the Original). The jump is significant — most lifters will lose 30 to 40 percent of their working weight with the Extreme. Start with the Original unless you already have above-average grip strength. The Original is the better long-term training tool for most people.

**Fat Gripz vs Generic Thick Bar Grips:** Amazon has several $15-20 knockoffs. They work in the short term, but the rubber is typically softer and will split at the seam within a few months. Fat Gripz's military-grade compound is genuinely superior — this is not marketing fluff. The knockoffs also tend to have a looser fit on bars, which can slip during heavy pulls.
