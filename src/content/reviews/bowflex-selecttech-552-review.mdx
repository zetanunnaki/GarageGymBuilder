The Bowflex SelectTech 552 has been the best-selling adjustable dumbbell for over a decade. But in a market that's exploded with competition, is it still the one to buy? After 12 months of daily use, here's our take.

## The Good

The dial mechanism is still the fastest weight change system on the market. You can go from 10 lbs to 40 lbs in under 2 seconds. For supersets and circuit training, nothing beats it.

## 12-Month Durability Report

After a year of 5-day-per-week training, our pair is still functioning perfectly. The dials still click cleanly, no plates have loosened, and the cradles are solid. We did notice minor cosmetic wear on the rubber grip, but nothing structural.

The key to longevity: **never drop them**. Set them down controlled, every time.

## Who Should Buy These?

The SelectTech 552 is perfect for home gym owners who want versatile dumbbell training without dedicating half their garage to a dumbbell rack. If your heaviest dumbbell exercises stay under 52.5 lbs, these are all you need.

## Compared to Alternatives

**Bowflex 552 vs PowerBlock Elite 50:** The PowerBlock is more compact, slightly cheaper, and more durable if dropped. But the dial system on the Bowflex is faster and more intuitive than PowerBlock's pin system. If speed between sets matters to you, Bowflex wins. If you train heavy and want something you can be less careful with, PowerBlock is the safer bet.

**Bowflex 552 vs NUOBELL 50:** The NUOBELL looks and feels like a traditional dumbbell, which is its biggest advantage. It is shorter at heavier weights and has a more natural grip. But it tops out at 50 lbs (vs 52.5) and costs significantly more. The Bowflex remains the better value.

## 6-Month Durability Update

At the six-month mark, we noticed the rubber grip texture had smoothed out slightly on both dumbbells. Functionally irrelevant, but worth noting if you train with sweaty hands — chalk or gloves help. The dial mechanism still clicks with the same precision it did on day one. No looseness in the cradles, no rattling plates, no cosmetic cracking on the plastic housing.

One thing we tested deliberately: re-racking at odd angles. If you drop a dumbbell back into the cradle slightly off-center, it can occasionally fail to seat a plate correctly. Always re-rack centered and flat. This is the single biggest maintenance habit that will keep these working for years.
