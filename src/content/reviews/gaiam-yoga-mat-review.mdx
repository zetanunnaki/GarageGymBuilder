## 3-Month Durability Update

Three months of daily use for stretching, ab work, and post-lifting mobility sessions. The mat surface has held up without tearing, peeling, or losing its texture. We did notice the mat absorbs sweat and can get slippery during intense floor work — a quick wipe with a damp cloth between exercises solves this.

The 10mm thickness is the star feature. Knee-down exercises like lunges, ab rollouts, and planks feel noticeably more comfortable compared to thinner 6mm mats. The carry strap is basic but functional — it keeps the mat rolled tight for storage against a wall or in a closet.

## Compared to Alternatives

**Gaiam Essentials vs BalanceFrom 1/2-inch Yoga Mat:** The BalanceFrom is very similar in thickness and price, with a slightly tackier surface. The Gaiam has better color options and a more consistent foam density edge-to-edge. Both are solid — this comes down to personal preference and which one is in stock.

**Gaiam Essentials (10mm) vs Manduka PRO (6mm):** The Manduka PRO ($80+) is the industry standard for serious yoga practitioners. It is denser, more durable, and provides better ground feel for standing poses. But for home gym athletes who mainly need cushioning for stretches and floor work, the Gaiam at one-quarter the price does everything you need.
