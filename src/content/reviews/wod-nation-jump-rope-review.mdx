## Speed Rope vs Weighted Rope

There are two camps in jump rope: speed and weighted. They serve different goals.

**Speed rope (this rope):**
- Fast rotations
- Best for double-unders, conditioning, footwork
- Ideal for boxers, CrossFitters, athletes

**Weighted rope:**
- Slower, harder rotations
- Builds shoulder and forearm strength
- Best for strength conditioning

For most people, a speed rope is the right starting point. You can always add a weighted rope later.

## Sizing Your Rope

Before your first jump:
1. Stand on the middle of the rope with one foot
2. Pull the handles up
3. Handles should reach your armpits (slightly below if you're advanced)

Most people leave the rope too long. Shorter = faster.

## Common Mistakes

1. **Jumping too high** — minimal clearance, just clear the rope
2. **Using your arms** — wrist rotation only, arms stay still
3. **Looking down** — eyes forward, the rope finds your feet
4. **Bouncing twice per swing** — one jump per rotation, smooth rhythm

Master single unders before attempting double-unders. Patience beats injuries.
