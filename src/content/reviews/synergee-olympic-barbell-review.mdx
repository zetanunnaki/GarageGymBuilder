## Build Quality

Unboxing reveals a well-packaged bar. The finish is black phosphate — a matte black coating that's more rust-resistant than bare steel but softer than chrome. The sleeves spin freely out of the box, which is a sign of quality bearings.

The knurling is medium-aggressive — secure grip without tearing hands during high-rep sets. Both center knurling (for back squats) and dual knurling marks (powerlifting and Olympic positions) are clearly visible.

## Needle Bearings: The Game Changer

The biggest upgrade from a budget bar is the needle bearings. Budget bars use bushings, which are fine for squats and bench but sluggish on cleans and snatches. Needle bearings allow the sleeves to spin quickly during Olympic lifts, reducing stress on your wrists and elbows.

For the $80-100 premium over a bushing bar, the needle bearings are worth every penny if you do any Olympic lifting.

## Care & Maintenance

The black phosphate finish requires more maintenance than chrome or cerakote:

1. **Weekly:** Wipe down shaft and sleeves with dry cloth
2. **Monthly:** Apply 3-in-1 oil to the shaft and sleeves
3. **After humid days:** Oil immediately to prevent rust
4. **Chalk residue:** Brush with nylon brush, then re-oil

Done correctly, the bar will last decades.
