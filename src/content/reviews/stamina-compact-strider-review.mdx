## 3-Month Durability Update

Three months of near-daily use, averaging 20 to 30 minutes per session. The striding motion remains smooth with no squeaking or grinding. The resistance dial still works consistently across its range. The LCD display is functional and accurate for tracking basic metrics.

We placed ours on a BalanceFrom puzzle mat to prevent sliding on the concrete garage floor — this solved the sliding issue completely. Without a mat underneath, it will creep forward on smooth surfaces during use. The foot pedals show no wear on the textured surface, and the hydraulic cylinder that provides resistance shows no signs of leaking.

## Compared to Alternatives

**Stamina InMotion vs Cubii JR2:** The Cubii ($149) is the premium under-desk elliptical with Bluetooth tracking and a quieter mechanism. It costs nearly twice as much and provides a similar range of motion. For basic low-impact cardio while working from home, the Stamina does the same job at a better price. The Cubii is worth the premium only if you need app-based tracking.

**Stamina InMotion vs Walking:** Walking outside is free and provides better cardiovascular benefit due to full range of motion and weight-bearing movement. The Stamina strider is for situations where walking is not practical — during desk work, in bad weather, or for people with joint issues that make full-stride walking painful. It supplements rather than replaces real walking.
