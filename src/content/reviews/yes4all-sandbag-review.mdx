## Why a Sandbag Belongs in Your Gym

Sandbags train you in ways barbells can't:

1. **Shifting load** — sand moves as you lift, building stabilizers
2. **Awkward grip** — multiple handles force grip variations
3. **Real-world strength** — translates to lifting awkward objects (kids, furniture, ground game)
4. **Conditioning** — sandbag complexes are brutal cardio
5. **Forgiving on joints** — softer than steel, less impact

Strongmen and combat athletes have used sandbags for centuries. Most home gym owners ignore them. They shouldn't.

## What to Fill It With

You have three options:

### 1. Pea Gravel ($15 from Home Depot)
**Pros:** Cleanest, doesn't compress, won't clump
**Cons:** More expensive than sand, can be loud
**Best for:** Indoor training

### 2. Play Sand ($5 from Home Depot)
**Pros:** Cheapest, most realistic feel
**Cons:** Can leak through small tears, gets damp
**Best for:** Garage gym training

### 3. Steel Shot ($30+)
**Pros:** Densest fill, smallest size
**Cons:** Most expensive
**Best for:** Heavy bags (150+ lbs)

For most users, pea gravel is the right choice. Cleaner, more durable, and only slightly more expensive than sand.

## Best Sandbag Exercises

### Strength
1. **Bear hug squats** — squat with sandbag held to chest
2. **Sandbag clean** — pick from floor to shoulders
3. **Sandbag press** — overhead press with awkward load
4. **Sandbag rows** — bent-over rows with multiple grips

### Conditioning
5. **Sandbag carries** — walk with the bag in various positions
6. **Sandbag get-ups** — Turkish get-up variation
7. **Over-the-shoulder throws** — explosive power
8. **Sandbag burpees** — burpee with a sandbag pickup at the top

### The Brutal Workout
- 5 rounds: 10 squats, 10 cleans, 10 presses, 50 yard carry
- Rest as needed between rounds
- Total time: 15-25 minutes of suffering
