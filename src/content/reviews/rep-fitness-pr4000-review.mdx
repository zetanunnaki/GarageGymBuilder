The REP PR-4000 sits in that dangerous sweet spot where you're paying enough to expect quality but not so much that you're in Rogue territory. After 3 months of daily use, here's our verdict.

## First Impressions

The box arrived heavy. Really heavy. That's a good sign with power racks — you want mass in the frame. Assembly took about 2 hours with two people and required a socket wrench set.

<ProductCard productId="rep-pr-4000" badge="Top Pick" />

## Build Quality

The 3x3" 11-gauge steel uprights are the same spec as racks twice this price. There's zero flex during heavy squats and the J-cups are UHMW-lined, meaning no barbell damage.

The REP PR-4000 gives you 90% of the Rogue experience at 75% of the price. The main trade-off is the attachment ecosystem — Rogue's is larger, but REP is catching up fast.

## Who Should Buy the PR-4000?

The PR-4000 is ideal for intermediate to advanced lifters who want a rack they'll never outgrow but don't want to pay the Rogue tax. If you're squatting 400+ and plan to add attachments over time, this is your rack.

## Final Verdict

**Rating: 4.7/5** — The REP PR-4000 is the best value in the mid-range power rack market. Period.
