## Why Every Lifter Needs Hyperextensions

Back extensions are one of the safest and most effective lower-back exercises ever invented. They:

- **Strengthen the erectors** (the muscles that prevent back injury)
- **Build glute and hamstring endurance** without spinal compression
- **Reduce back pain** through targeted strengthening
- **Improve deadlift lockout** by training end-range hip extension

If you deadlift regularly, you should be doing back extensions. They prevent the injuries that end careers.

## Best Exercises on a Roman Chair

### 1. Back Extensions
The classic. Lower your torso, then raise back to neutral. 3-4 sets of 12-15 reps. Hold a plate at your chest for added resistance.

### 2. Glute-Focused Hyperextensions
Same movement but with a slight upper-back rounding to shift focus from erectors to glutes. Excellent for building strong glutes without squatting.

### 3. Reverse Hyperextensions
Lie face-down with legs hanging off. Lift legs up and back. Targets glutes and hamstrings without spinal loading.

### 4. Roman Chair Sit-Ups
Hook your feet, lower your torso back, then sit back up. Trains abs through a much greater range of motion than crunches.

### 5. Side Hyperextensions
Lie on your side, hook one leg, raise your torso laterally. Trains the obliques.

## Programming Suggestions

**For Strength (3 days/week):**
- Back Extensions: 4x10 with weight
- Reverse Hyperextensions: 3x12 bodyweight

**For Hypertrophy (2 days/week):**
- Glute-Focused Hyperextensions: 4x15
- Roman Chair Sit-Ups: 3x15

**For Rehab (Daily):**
- Bodyweight Back Extensions: 2x15
- Hold for 5 sec at the top
