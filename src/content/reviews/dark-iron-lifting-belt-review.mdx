## What a Lifting Belt Actually Does

A belt does NOT support your back directly. It gives you something to brace against — increasing intra-abdominal pressure, which stabilizes your spine. The result: heavier lifts and reduced injury risk on max-effort sets.

You don't need a belt for warm-ups or sets under 70% of your max. You DO need one for heavy singles, doubles, and any set above 85% of your max.

## Sizing Tips

The most common complaint is sizing. The size chart is technically accurate but most users report needing to size up:

- **Measure your waist at belt height** (not pants size)
- **Order one size larger than the chart suggests**
- **The buckle should be on the middle hole** when fitted properly

## Single vs Double Prong

This belt has a single prong. Premium belts (Inzer, Pioneer) often have double prongs for extra security. Differences:

- **Single prong:** Easier to put on/off, slightly less secure
- **Double prong:** More secure but slower to adjust

For most lifters, single prong is fine. Double prong matters more for elite powerlifters.
