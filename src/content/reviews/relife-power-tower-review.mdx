The RELIFE Power Tower is the entry point for home calisthenics. At $129, it is the cheapest power tower we would actually recommend. ## What You Get for $129

The RELIFE delivers four bodyweight training stations:

1. **Pull-up bar** — straight bar at adjustable height, accommodating athletes from roughly 5'3" to 6'2"
2. **Dip handles** — padded parallel bars angled slightly inward at approximately 21 inches apart
3. **Captain's chair** — arm pads and backrest for knee raises and leg raises
4. **Push-up handles** — elevated grips at the base for deeper push-ups

The rated weight capacity is 400 lbs, which is generous for this price. For reference, the [Stamina 1690](/reviews/stamina-power-tower-review/) costs $119 but is only rated to 250 lbs. That extra 150 lbs of capacity gives the RELIFE meaningful headroom for heavier athletes or light weighted calisthenics.

## Assembly

Assembly took us about 50 minutes with one person. The RELIFE is simpler than larger power towers because it has fewer adjustable components. The hardware comes in labeled bags, and the instruction sheet (not a full manual) covers the process in about 12 steps.

The most common assembly complaint we have seen in user reviews is misaligned bolt holes. We experienced this on one upright bracket — the holes were off by about 1mm. The fix was to leave surrounding bolts loose until everything was threaded, then tighten in sequence. This is standard procedure for tubular steel fitness equipment.

**Assembly tip:** Tighten all structural bolts to manufacturer spec with a torque wrench, not just hand-tight. Loose bolts are the primary cause of wobble and squeaking during use.

## The Training Experience

### Pull-Ups

The pull-up bar is a straight cylindrical bar without multi-grip handles. The knurling is moderate — enough grip for dry hands, but add chalk or grip tape for sweaty sessions. We tested overhand pull-ups, chin-ups, and wide-grip pull-ups with testers at 155 lbs, 180 lbs, and 205 lbs.

At 155 lbs, the tower felt rock-solid during strict pull-ups with zero perceptible sway. At 180 lbs, there was a slight front-to-back rock during each rep — not alarming but noticeable. At 205 lbs, the sway was more pronounced and we would recommend placing the tower against a wall for added stability at this weight.

Kipping pull-ups at any weight caused excessive lateral movement. The base geometry is not designed for side-to-side dynamic loading. Strict form only on this tower.

### Dips

Dips are the RELIFE's strongest movement. The vertical loading travels straight through the frame, which is its most rigid axis. All three testers performed dips without stability concerns. The foam-padded handles are 2.5 inches thick — comfortable for sets of 10-15 reps but noticeably thinner than the Sportsroyals (3 inches). Longer dip sessions (5+ sets) can cause forearm fatigue from the handle compression.

The handle spacing of approximately 21 inches is slightly narrow. Broader-shouldered athletes (50+ inch chest) may feel restricted. For average-build athletes, the spacing works for both chest-emphasis (forward lean) and tricep-emphasis (upright) dips.

### Knee and Leg Raises

The captain's chair position works as expected. The arm pads support your forearms while you raise your knees or legs. The backrest prevents excessive swinging. One detail worth noting: the backrest is fixed (not adjustable like the Sportsroyals), so very short athletes (under 5'4") may find the arm pads sitting slightly too high for comfortable knee raises.

### Push-Up Handles

The base handles elevate your hands about 3.5 inches, adding range of motion to the push-up. The handles are fixed at roughly shoulder width. Functional and simple — nothing special, but a nice inclusion at this price.

## Beginner Bodyweight Program Using the RELIFE

If you are new to calisthenics, here is a progression program using only this tower:

### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-4)
- Negative pull-ups (jump up, lower slowly over 5 seconds) x 5 reps x 3 sets
- Bench dips (feet on floor, hands on dip handles) x 10 reps x 3 sets
- Knee raises from captain's chair x 10 reps x 3 sets
- Push-ups on handles x 10 reps x 3 sets
- Rest 90 seconds between sets. Train 3 days per week.

### Phase 2: Building (Weeks 5-8)
- Assisted pull-ups (band around bar and foot) x 5 reps x 4 sets
- Full bodyweight dips x 6 reps x 3 sets
- Leg raises from captain's chair x 8 reps x 3 sets
- Push-ups on handles x 15 reps x 3 sets
- Rest 75 seconds between sets. Train 3-4 days per week.

### Phase 3: Full Bodyweight (Weeks 9-12)
- Strict pull-ups x max reps x 4 sets
- Dips x 10 reps x 4 sets
- Hanging knee raises from bar x 12 reps x 3 sets
- Deficit push-ups on handles x 15 reps x 3 sets
- Rest 60 seconds between sets. Train 4 days per week.

### Phase 4: Weighted (Weeks 13+)
Add a weight vest or dip belt in 5 lb increments. The 400 lb capacity gives you room to grow.

## 3-Month Durability Report

After three months of training 3-4 days per week with three rotating testers:

- **Frame:** No structural concerns. All welds intact. No visible bending or stress marks.
- **Bolts:** Two bolts loosened at the 6-week mark — retightened without issue. We recommend monthly bolt checks.
- **Padding:** Arm pads compressed approximately 20% from new. Back pad compressed about 15%. Still functional but noticeably thinner than when new. We expect the pads to need replacement or supplementation (wrap with grip tape or pipe insulation) by the 12-month mark.
- **Pull-up bar:** No bending. Knurling still grippy.
- **Base caps:** Holding their position on concrete. One cap cracked slightly at the edge but remains functional.

## RELIFE vs Sportsroyals: Which Should You Buy?

This is the most common comparison, and the answer depends on your weight and plans:

**Buy the RELIFE ($129) if:**
- You weigh under 180 lbs
- You train with bodyweight only (no weighted vest or dip belt)
- Budget is a priority
- You want the most compact option

**Buy the [Sportsroyals](/reviews/sportsroyals-power-tower-review/) ($149) if:**
- You weigh over 180 lbs
- You plan to add weight via vest or dip belt
- You want thicker padding and adjustable backrest
- You want the extra 50 lbs of rated capacity

The $20 difference is small. If you are on the fence, the Sportsroyals is the safer long-term choice.

## Who Should Buy the RELIFE Power Tower

**Buy it if:**
- You want a complete bodyweight station under $130
- You are under 200 lbs and train with strict form
- You need compact equipment for a small space
- You are building a calisthenics foundation and want to progress at home

**Skip it if:**
- You weigh over 220 lbs — the stability margin gets thin

- You do CrossFit-style kipping pull-ups — this tower is not stable enough
