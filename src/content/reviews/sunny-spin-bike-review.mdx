Most budget spin bikes use friction-pad resistance — a felt pad pressing against the flywheel. The Sunny SF-B1805 uses magnetic resistance instead, which means zero contact, zero brake-pad wear, and near-silent operation. At $369, it sits between the throwaway $150 Amazon bikes and the $1,400+ Peloton. After four months of daily use, here is where it lands.

## The Ride Feel

The 44 lb chrome flywheel is the star of this bike. Heavier flywheels produce smoother momentum, and 44 lbs is substantial for this price range. Pedal stroke feels consistent through the full rotation with no dead spots at the top or bottom. Standing climbs feel stable — the frame does not rock or flex under a 200 lb rider pushing hard.

The magnetic resistance dial offers a smooth range from easy spinning to a grind that will have your quads burning in under a minute. There are no numbered levels on the dial, which means you cannot replicate exact resistance settings between workouts. You learn the positions by feel over time.

## Build Quality and Assembly

Assembly takes roughly 45 minutes with the included tools. The main frame comes pre-assembled — you attach the handlebars, seat post, pedals, and stabilizer feet. The instructions are adequate but not great; a YouTube assembly video helps.

The steel frame feels overbuilt for the price. Welds are clean, the powder coat is thick, and the leveling feet compensate for uneven garage floors. After four months of daily sweat sessions, there is zero rust on the flywheel, zero looseness in the crank, and zero degradation in the resistance system. Magnetic resistance has no wearing parts, so this bike should maintain its ride quality for years.

## Noise Levels

This is where the SF-B1805 genuinely shines. Magnetic resistance produces no friction noise. The belt drive is silent. The only sound you hear is the whoosh of the flywheel and your own breathing. We measured roughly 45 dB at moderate intensity — quieter than a normal conversation. If you live in an apartment or train early morning, this bike will not disturb anyone.

## Who Should Buy This

**Buy the SF-B1805 if:**
- You want a quiet spin bike for apartment or early-morning use
- You follow along with app-based classes (Peloton app, Zwift, Apple Fitness+) and need a reliable bike under the content
- You want the durability of magnetic resistance without paying Peloton prices
- You ride 3-7 days per week and need something that holds up

**Skip it if:**
- You want a built-in screen with live classes — the [Schwinn 170 upright](/reviews/schwinn-upright-bike-review/) has dual LCDs
- You are over 300 lbs — the weight limit is 275 lbs
- You want precise, repeatable resistance levels with numbered settings

## SF-B1805 vs Peloton

The Peloton ($1,445+) gives you a 22" touchscreen, live classes, and leaderboard motivation. The Sunny gives you 90% of the ride quality at 25% of the price. If you already subscribe to a cycling app on your tablet, the Sunny with a tablet holder replicates most of the Peloton experience. What you lose is the integrated screen and social features.
