## Sample Training Program

**Day 1 — Push**
- Ring dips: 4 x 8-12
- Push-ups (parallette): 3 x 15-20
- Handstand push-ups (wall-assisted): 3 x 5-8
- Ring push-ups: 3 x 10-15

**Day 2 — Pull**
- Pull-ups (weighted if needed): 5 x 5-8
- Ring rows: 4 x 12-15
- Muscle-up negatives: 3 x 3-5
- Band-assisted front lever holds: 3 x 10-15 sec

**Day 3 — Legs & Core**
- Pistol squat progressions: 4 x 5-8 each leg
- Weighted vest squats: 3 x 20
- L-sit holds (parallettes): 4 x 15-30 sec
- Hanging leg raises (power tower): 4 x 10-15
- Hollow body holds: 3 x 30-45 sec

**Day 4 — Rest + Mobility** (foam rolling, stretching)

Repeat on a 4-day cycle.

## Why Calisthenics?

Calisthenics builds relative strength — how strong you are relative to your bodyweight. This translates directly to:
- Better athletic performance
- Lower injury risk
- Functional movement patterns
- Joint health (bodyweight training is lower-impact than heavy barbell work)
- Impressively aesthetic physiques
