## Apartment-Friendly Rules

### Noise
- No dropping weights — ever
- No jumping exercises during quiet hours
- Use rubber mats under everything
- Control the eccentric on every rep

### Space
- Everything must store in a closet or under a bed
- The FLYBIRD bench folds flat (17" when folded)
- Resistance bands hang on a door hook
- Jump rope fits in a drawer

### Floor Protection
- Use a thick exercise mat (6mm+) under all training
- If you have hardwood: add a rubber mat under the exercise mat
- Never place dumbbells directly on wood or tile

## What You Can Train

This apartment setup covers every major muscle group:

**Chest:** Dumbbell bench press (flat/incline), band push-ups, floor flyes
**Back:** Pull-ups/chin-ups, dumbbell rows, band pull-downs
**Shoulders:** Dumbbell press, lateral raises, band face pulls
**Legs:** Goblet squats, Bulgarian split squats, band leg curls, lunges
**Arms:** Dumbbell curls, band pushdowns, close-grip floor press
**Core:** Ab wheel, dead bugs, planks, band pallof press
**Cardio:** Jump rope, burpees (daytime only), band circuits

## Sample Apartment Workout (30 Minutes)

1. Jump rope warm-up — 3 minutes
2. Goblet squat — 3x12
3. Dumbbell bench press — 3x10
4. Pull-ups — 3x max
5. Dumbbell row — 3x10
6. Band lateral raise — 3x15
7. Ab wheel — 3x10
8. Band pull-apart — 3x20

## Upgrade Path

When budget allows:
1. **Better dumbbells** ($300-430) — Bowflex 552 for the 5-52.5 lb range
2. **Suspension trainer** ($30-50) — TRX-style for bodyweight rowing and pressing
3. **Gymnastics rings** ($30) — hang from pull-up bar for dips and rows
4. **Heavier bands** ($20) — for banded squats and deadlifts

## The Bottom Line

An apartment gym won't replace a full garage gym. But for $300, you get enough equipment to build muscle, improve conditioning, and maintain fitness without leaving your home. The key is consistency — a $300 setup used 4 days a week beats a $5,000 gym used once a month.
