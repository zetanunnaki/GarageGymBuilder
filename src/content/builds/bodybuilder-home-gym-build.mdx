Bodybuilders have different equipment priorities than powerlifters. You need variety, isolation work, high volume capability, and the ability to hit every muscle from multiple angles. This build gives you a complete bodybuilding home gym for under $1,500 — everything from compound lifts to focused isolation work.

## The Bodybuilder's Equipment Hierarchy

A bodybuilder's gear priorities:

1. **Adjustable dumbbells** (the king of bodybuilding equipment)
2. **Power rack with cable system** (compounds + isolation cables)
3. **Adjustable bench** (multiple angles for chest)
4. **Pull-up bar with attachments** (back development)
5. **Dip belt** (weighted dips and pull-ups for advanced)
6. **Hyperextension bench** (lower back, glutes, hamstrings)
7. **Ab wheel** (core training that beats crunches)

This build covers all 7. Optimized for hypertrophy.

## What's Different from Powerlifting Build

The bodybuilder build prioritizes:
- **Cable system** over heavy barbell (more isolation options)
- **Dumbbells** over heavy plates (better unilateral training)
- **Variety** over absolute strength
- **Volume tools** (ab wheel, dip belt, hyperextensions) over max strength tools

## The Bodybuilder's Push/Pull/Legs Split

This program uses only the build above:

### Push Day (Chest, Shoulders, Triceps)
- Cable Crossovers: 4x12
- Dumbbell Incline Press: 4x10
- Dumbbell Shoulder Press: 4x10
- Cable Lateral Raises: 4x12
- Cable Tricep Pushdowns: 4x12
- Dumbbell Skull Crushers: 3x10

### Pull Day (Back, Biceps)
- Lat Pulldown: 4x10
- Cable Row: 4x10
- Weighted Pull-Ups: 3x to failure
- Dumbbell Row: 4x10 each arm
- Cable Bicep Curls: 4x12
- Hammer Curls: 3x12
- Hyperextensions: 3x15

### Leg Day
- Goblet Squat: 4x10
- Romanian Deadlift: 4x8
- Bulgarian Split Squat: 3x10 each leg
- Walking Lunges: 3x10 each leg
- Cable Pull-Through: 3x12
- Calf Raises: 4x15

### Run 6 days/week with 1 rest day, repeat the cycle.
