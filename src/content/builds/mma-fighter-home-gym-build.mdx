MMA fighters need a different kind of home gym. Combat sports demand explosive power, conditioning, awkward strength, and grip endurance — not pure powerlifting or bodybuilding equipment. This build gives you a complete combat sports home gym for under $1,000 from Amazon and Walmart.

## The MMA Fighter's Equipment Hierarchy

A combat athlete's gear priorities:

1. **Heavy bag** (striking conditioning)
2. **Kettlebells** (explosive power, grip strength)
3. **Sandbag** (awkward functional strength)
4. **Weight vest** (rucking, conditioning, weighted calisthenics)
5. **Jump rope** (footwork, conditioning)
6. **Pull-up bar** (grip and pulling strength)
7. **Power rack with safety bars** (heavy strength work, optional)

This build focuses on combat-specific tools, not bodybuilding equipment.

## What This Build Does Best

### Striking Day
- Heavy bag rounds (5 x 3 minutes)
- Jump rope warm-up (3 rounds)
- Shadow boxing
- Burpees and push-ups for conditioning

### Conditioning Day
- Kettlebell swings (5 x 30 reps, EMOM)
- Sandbag carries (200m total)
- Weight vest pull-ups (3 x to failure)
- Slam ball throws (5 x 10)

### Strength Day
- Kettlebell clean and press (5 x 5)
- Sandbag bear hug squats (4 x 10)
- Weighted pull-ups (5 x 5)
- Ring dips (4 x 8)
- Hyperextensions (alternative if you add a Roman chair)

### Recovery / Mobility Day
- Foam rolling (15 minutes)
- Mobility flow (yoga or dynamic stretching)
- Light jump rope
- Visualization training

## The Combat Athlete's Weekly Plan

### Monday: Strength + Striking
- 30 min strength (kettlebell, sandbag, ring work)
- 30 min heavy bag work

### Tuesday: Pure Conditioning
- 5 rounds of 1 min hard / 1 min rest
- Mix: kettlebell swings, slam balls, burpees, jump rope

### Wednesday: Recovery
- Foam rolling, mobility, light cardio

### Thursday: Strength + Striking
- 30 min strength
- 30 min heavy bag work

### Friday: Conditioning
- Same as Tuesday but different exercises

### Saturday: Long Session
- 60-90 min combination of all the above

### Sunday: Off

## Why No Power Rack or Barbell?

Most MMA fighters don't need to bench press 300 lbs or squat 500 lbs. They need:
- Explosive power (kettlebells, slam balls)
- Awkward strength (sandbags, carries)
- Conditioning (jump rope, bag work)
- Functional pulling (rings, pull-ups)
- Recovery tools (foam rollers)

A power rack and barbell are luxury additions, not necessities, for combat athletes. Skip them and buy combat-specific tools instead.

If you want both, see our [crossfit home gym build](/builds/crossfit-home-gym-build/) which adds barbell work.

## What's Missing

The $1,000 MMA build doesn't include:

- **Wrestling/grappling mats** ($300+ for serious tatami) — buy locally
- **Speed bag** ($50-100) — optional skill tool
- **Double-end bag** ($50-100) — optional accuracy tool
- **Squat rack** ($330+) — only if you want barbell strength training
- **Olympic barbell** ($200+) — only if you add a rack

These are nice-to-haves. Focus on the basics first.
