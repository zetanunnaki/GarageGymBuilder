$500 won't buy you a dream gym, but it will buy you a gym that kicks the crap out of any Planet Fitness membership. Here's how to spend it.

## The $500 Build List

| Equipment | Cost |
|-----------|------|
| Barbell + 300 lb Plate Set | $249 |
| Horse Stall Mats (x2) | $100 |
| Squat Stands (pair) | $120 |
| **Total** | **$469** |

That leaves ~$30 for chalk, clips, and a foam roller.

## The Barbell Set: CAP Barbell 300 lb Olympic Set

At this price point, the CAP 300 lb set is unbeatable. You get a 7-foot Olympic barbell and 255 lbs of cast iron plates. The bar won't win any awards, but it'll hold up for years of training under 400 lbs.

## What About a Rack?

At $500, a full power rack stretches the budget too thin. Instead, we recommend a pair of squat stands. They're less versatile but get you squatting safely for $120 instead of $300.

## What You Can Train

Even with this minimal setup, you can perform:

- **Squats** (back squat, front squat)
- **Bench Press** (floor press without a bench)
- **Deadlift** (conventional, sumo, Romanian)
- **Overhead Press** (standing)
- **Barbell Rows** (bent-over, Pendlay)
- **Curls, Skullcrushers, Lunges** and more

## Upgrade Priority

When you can invest more:

1. **Power rack** ($300) — safety and pull-ups
2. **Adjustable bench** ($140) — incline/decline pressing
3. **Extra plates** ($150) — you'll outgrow 300 lbs

## The Bottom Line

$500 gets you into the game. It's not fancy, but it covers every major compound movement. Start here, prove the commitment, then upgrade piece by piece.
