CrossFit demands more equipment variety than any other training style. You need barbells, kettlebells, gymnastic rings, jump ropes, plyo boxes, slam balls — basically a complete CrossFit affiliate at home. The good news: you can build a real CrossFit home gym for under $2,000 if you know what to prioritize.

## The CrossFit Equipment Hierarchy

CrossFit's "constantly varied" methodology requires:

1. **Power rack** (squat, bench, press, pull-ups)
2. **Olympic barbell** (Olympic lifts)
3. **Bumper plates** (drop-safe Olympic lifts) — partially covered by mixed cast iron set
4. **Kettlebells** (swings, snatches, get-ups)
5. **Gymnastic rings** (muscle-ups, ring rows, dips)
6. **Jump rope** (double-unders)
7. **Plyo box** (box jumps, step-ups)
8. **Slam ball** (wall balls, slams)
9. **Pull-up bar** (covered by power rack)

This build covers all of it. Real CrossFit at home.

## What's Missing

The $2,000 CrossFit build doesn't include:

- **Rower** ($250-1,000) — Concept2 RowErg is the standard but pricey. Sunny SF-RW5515 ($250) is a budget alternative
- **Air bike** ($400-750) — Assault AirBike for "hero" workouts and conditioning
- **Bumper plates** ($150-300 for a pair of 45s) — for safe overhead drops
- **Olympic shoes** ($100-150) — improves squat and Olympic lift form

Add these as budget allows. Many CrossFitters train without them for years.
