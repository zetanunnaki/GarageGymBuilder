The $500 build is great for absolute beginners. The $1,000 build adds quality but spreads the budget thin. The $750 sweet spot is what we recommend for most home gym builders — enough budget to buy the right rack and barbell without compromising on the essentials.

This guide breaks down exactly what to buy.

## Why $750 Is the Sweet Spot

At $500 you have to make compromises (cheapest possible rack, no extras). At $1,000 you have flexibility but most beginners don't need it yet. At $750 you get:

- A real power rack with safety bars (not a half rack or squat stand)
- A complete Olympic weight set (bar + plates + collars)
- A real adjustable bench
- Quality flooring under the rack
- Room for one accessory or upgrade

This setup handles 95% of home gym training for years.

## Hitting $750 Exactly

To hit $750, you need to make one of three trade-offs:

### Option A: Skip the Bench Initially (-$110)
**Total: $720**
- Power rack: $330
- Weight set: $340
- Flooring: $50
- **Add bench in 1-2 months as funds allow**

This is the option we recommend for most builders. You can squat, deadlift, overhead press, and do pull-ups without a bench. Add it when you can.

### Option B: Used Equipment (-$80)
**Total: $750**
- Used power rack from Facebook Marketplace: $200-250
- New CAP weight set: $340
- New FLYBIRD bench: $110
- Flooring: $50
- **Total: ~$750**

Used racks are often half the price of new and just as functional. See our [used equipment buying guide](/guides/buying-used-gym-equipment/).

### Option C: Spring Specials (-$80)
**Total: $750**

Many of these items go on sale during major holidays:
- Black Friday: 20-30% off Fitness Reality, Bowflex
- Memorial Day: 15-20% off Amazon fitness gear
- Prime Day: 10-25% off most brands

Time your purchases around sales to hit budget.

## What's Missing

The $750 setup is complete for strength training, but lacks:

1. **Adjustable dumbbells** — for unilateral and accessory work ($150-430)
2. **Cardio machine** — rower or bike ($250-750)
3. **Cable system** — pull-down and row attachments ($100-250)
4. **Specialty bars** — trap bar, safety squat bar ($130-300)

Add these as your budget grows. None are essential for a beginner.

## Sample 12-Week Beginner Program

This program uses only the $750 setup:

### Week 1-4: Build a Base

**Day A (Monday/Friday):**
- Back Squat: 3x5
- Bench Press: 3x5
- Bent-Over Row: 3x5
- Plank: 3x30 sec

**Day B (Wednesday):**
- Deadlift: 1x5
- Overhead Press: 3x5
- Pull-Ups (or assisted): 3x6
- Dead Bug: 3x10 each side

### Week 5-8: Add Volume

Same lifts, add a fourth set on each:
- Squat: 4x5
- Bench: 4x5
- Row: 4x5
- Press: 4x5

### Week 9-12: Add Accessories

Add 2 accessory exercises after main work:
- Bicep curls: 3x10
- Tricep extensions: 3x10
- Romanian deadlift: 3x8
- Bulgarian split squat: 3x8

After 12 weeks, you'll be much stronger than when you started, and you'll know whether to add equipment or just keep training.
