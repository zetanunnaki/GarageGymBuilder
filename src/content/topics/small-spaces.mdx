## Small Space Doesn't Mean Small Workout

If you live in an apartment, a tiny bedroom, or a studio, you can still build a serious home gym. The smallest functional setups fit in 50 square feet. With smart equipment choices and disciplined storage, you'll train as hard as anyone with a 2-car garage — and your gear will outlast theirs.

The trick is buying equipment that does multiple jobs and folds away when not in use.

## Space Tiers

### 50 sq ft (7' x 7') — The Apartment Corner

Fits adjustable dumbbells in a cradle, a foldable bench, a doorway pull-up bar, and resistance bands. No barbell training, no dropping weights, no jumping exercises. Works in any apartment, dorm room, or shared house. Budget: $300-500.

### 80 sq ft (8' x 10') — The Spare Bedroom

Adds a compact power rack and a 7' Olympic barbell. This is the minimum for real strength training. Most converted spare bedrooms hit this size. Budget: $750-1,000.

### 100-150 sq ft — The Single Garage Bay

Full power rack with attachments, complete plate set, dumbbells, room to do farmer's walks. The sweet spot for most home gym owners. Budget: $1,500-2,500.

## Apartment Training Without the Noise Complaints

Three rules for not getting evicted:

1. **Never drop weights.** Lower under control on every rep, including deadlifts. Adjustable dumbbells are designed for this — drop them and you void the warranty.
2. **No jumping exercises.** Skip box jumps, plyo, and most CrossFit benchmark workouts that include jumping. Substitute step-ups, broad jumps you walk back from, or low-intensity swings.
3. **Use horse stall mats.** $55 each from Tractor Supply. Reduces both noise transmission and floor damage.

A bonus rule: **train during waking hours.** No 10 PM training above your downstairs neighbor's bedroom. Be a reasonable human.

## Storage Is the Whole Game

In a small space, the equipment isn't visible most of the time. Plan for storage as carefully as you plan the equipment:

- **Vertical storage** — wall-mounted plate trees, dumbbell racks, and foldable benches save 50% of floor space.
- **Under-bed storage** — adjustable dumbbells fit under a bed frame.
- **Closet storage** — resistance bands, jump rope, foam roller, and yoga mat all fit in a single closet shelf.
- **Door anchors** — TRX, resistance bands, and door pull-up bars all use existing doors as anchor points. No installation needed.
