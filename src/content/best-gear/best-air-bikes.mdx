We tested 5 air bikes over 3 months of daily conditioning work. Here are the ones worth buying.

## What to Look For in an Air Bike

### Drive System
- **Belt-driven** (Rogue Echo) — quieter, smoother, zero maintenance
- **Chain-driven** (Assault, Schwinn) — louder, requires occasional lubrication

### Weight Capacity
Heavier bikes are more stable. The Echo Bike at 127 lbs barely moves during all-out sprints. Lighter bikes can rock and walk across the floor.

### Console
At minimum, you want calories, distance, and time. Heart rate monitoring is a bonus. Programmable workouts are nice but not essential.

### Seat Adjustability
Make sure the bike fits your height range. Most air bikes accommodate 5'2" to 6'4", but check the specs.

## Best Workouts for Air Bikes

1. **Tabata** — 20 sec on / 10 sec off x 8 rounds
2. **EMOM Calories** — Hit a calorie target every minute
3. **Steady State** — 20-30 min at conversational pace
4. **Assault Bike Ladder** — 2-4-6-8-10-8-6-4-2 calories with equal rest
