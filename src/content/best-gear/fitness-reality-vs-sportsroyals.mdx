## Who These Racks Are Actually For

Before diving into specs, it helps to understand who buys each rack. The ULTRA FUEGO Power Cage dominates the sub-$400 segment because it does one thing extremely well: it holds a barbell safely while you lift heavy. That singular focus is a feature, not a limitation. Thousands of garage gym owners have built impressive physiques inside an ULTRA FUEGO using nothing but a barbell, plates, and a bench.

The Sportsroyals Power Cage targets a different buyer entirely. It is built for the lifter who wants a complete cable-and-barbell training station without purchasing separate attachments over time. If your ideal session includes barbell squats followed by cable flyes, LAT pulldowns, and tricep pushdowns -- all without switching equipment -- the Sportsroyals delivers that workflow out of the box.

Neither rack is objectively better. The right choice depends on how you train today and how you plan to train twelve months from now.

## ULTRA FUEGO Power Cage -- Deep Dive

### Build Quality and Frame Construction

The ULTRA FUEGO uses 2x2-inch 14-gauge steel tubing throughout, which puts it in line with most racks in the $250-$400 range. The welds are clean, the powder coat holds up well against barbell racking, and the frame does not flex or sway during heavy squats up to 500 lbs in our testing. The 800 lb rated weight capacity is conservative -- the rack itself weighs approximately 120 lbs assembled, and the four upright posts distribute load evenly across a 50.5 x 46.5-inch footprint.

Hole spacing follows the standard 2x2-inch pattern on the uprights, which is one of the ULTRA FUEGO's biggest advantages. This universal spacing means that J-cups, spotter arms, dip attachments, and landmine mounts from dozens of third-party manufacturers fit without modification. That compatibility future-proofs your investment. If you want to add dip handles in six months or a landmine attachment in a year, the aftermarket ecosystem for 2x2-inch racks is enormous.

The included multi-grip pull-up bar offers four grip positions: wide overhand, narrow overhand, neutral, and angled neutral. It is welded directly to the frame rather than bolted, which eliminates any wobble during kipping or weighted pull-ups.

## Sportsroyals Power Cage -- Deep Dive

<ProductCard productId="sportsroyals-power-cage" badge="Most Features" />

### Build Quality and Frame Construction

The Sportsroyals steps up to heavy-duty steel construction -- the brand rates its frame at a 1,600 lb weight capacity, which is double the ULTRA FUEGO. The frame uses 2x2-inch tubing with thicker gauge steel throughout, and you can feel the difference the moment you grab an upright. There is virtually zero flex, even when pulling hard on the cable crossover arms.

At approximately 200 lbs assembled, the Sportsroyals is significantly heavier than the ULTRA FUEGO. That mass is an advantage for stability -- you are less likely to need floor bolts or sandbag ballast to keep the rack planted during aggressive movements. The footprint is modestly larger at 52 x 49 inches, but the functional difference is only about 1.5 inches wider and 2.5 inches deeper than the ULTRA FUEGO.

### The Cable System -- What $309.98 Actually Buys

The Sportsroyals' defining feature is its integrated cable crossover and LAT pulldown system. The high pulley arms extend outward from the top of the cage, allowing cable flyes, face pulls, and tricep pushdowns from multiple angles. The low pulley station handles cable rows, bicep curls, and upright rows. The LAT pulldown bar connects to a dedicated overhead pulley and comes included in the box.

Cable movement is smooth thanks to nylon-coated steel cables running through sealed bearings. The system uses standard Olympic weight plates loaded onto a loading pin rather than a fixed weight stack, which means your cable resistance scales with your plate collection. There is no artificial ceiling on how much weight you can load, though the pulley system has a practical limit around 200 lbs before friction becomes an issue.

### Real-World Training Performance

We tested the Sportsroyals over the same eight-week period using a hybrid program that combined barbell compounds with cable isolation work. Squats, bench press, and overhead press felt identical to the ULTRA FUEGO -- a rack is a rack when it comes to holding a barbell. The differentiator was what happened after the main lifts.

Being able to step from a set of heavy squats directly into cable pull-throughs without changing equipment is a genuine quality-of-life improvement. The LAT pulldown station with 135 lbs of plates loaded felt smooth and consistent through the full range of motion. Cable flyes at chest height were effective for a pump-focused finisher, though the fixed cable path does not replicate the feel of independent cable stacks found in commercial gyms.

The limitation we hit was cable resistance ceiling. Loading beyond 180-200 lbs on the pin introduced enough pulley friction that the movement felt grindy rather than smooth. For most home gym lifters doing cable work in the 50-150 lb range, this is a non-issue. For anyone who needs heavy LAT pulldowns above 200 lbs, a standalone cable machine is the better solution.

## Head-to-Head Spec Comparison

Understanding the raw numbers helps, but context matters more than specs in isolation. Here is how these two racks compare across every meaningful dimension.

**Frame and Steel Gauge.** The Sportsroyals uses thicker steel, which contributes to its higher weight capacity and greater rigidity. For lifters squatting under 500 lbs -- which includes the vast majority of home gym users -- the ULTRA FUEGO's 14-gauge steel is perfectly adequate. The thicker gauge becomes relevant only under extreme loads or if you plan to do aggressive kipping pull-ups that generate lateral force on the uprights.

**Weight Capacity.** The Sportsroyals' 1,600 lb rating versus the ULTRA FUEGO's 800 lbs sounds like a dramatic difference, but these numbers reflect the rack's total distributed load capacity, not the weight you can put on a single barbell. No home lifter is loading 800 lbs onto a bar inside either rack. Both racks will safely handle any realistic barbell load a non-competitive lifter will use.

**Footprint.** The Sportsroyals is only marginally larger in base dimensions (52 x 49 inches versus 50.5 x 46.5 inches). However, the cable crossover arms extend several inches beyond the base on each side, which effectively adds 12-18 inches of required clearance width. Plan your garage layout accordingly.

**Assembly.** The ULTRA FUEGO assembles in 60-90 minutes with one person and basic tools. The Sportsroyals demands 4-5 hours and a second pair of hands -- the cable routing alone takes over an hour if it is your first time. Expect frustration with the instruction manual, which is functional but not intuitive.

**Expandability.** The ULTRA FUEGO wins on aftermarket compatibility. Its standard hole spacing and minimal frame design mean you can add virtually any 2x2-inch rack attachment on the market. The Sportsroyals' integrated cable system limits where attachments can mount, and its non-standard rear frame geometry restricts some third-party accessories.

## The Bottom Line

The ULTRA FUEGO Power Cage remains the best pure power rack under $400 in 2026. It delivers exactly what a barbell-focused lifter needs -- a solid, stable cage with universal attachment compatibility and years of proven reliability. If cables are optional or something you might want eventually, start here.

The Sportsroyals Power Cage is the best all-in-one rack under $600. It trades some of the ULTRA FUEGO's simplicity and aftermarket flexibility for a feature set that would cost $300-500 to replicate with standalone cable equipment. If you already know you want cables and you have the space and patience for assembly, the Sportsroyals eliminates the need for a separate cable machine entirely.

Most first-time garage gym builders are better served by the ULTRA FUEGO. You save $160 upfront, you learn what attachments you actually use, and you maintain maximum flexibility to customize over time. The Sportsroyals is the smarter buy only for experienced lifters who have trained in commercial gyms, know exactly which cable exercises they rely on, and want to replicate that workflow at home from day one.
