**Quick Answer:** The 810XLT is the better pure rack for the money. The Sportsroyals is worth the $220 premium ONLY if you need built-in cables (LAT pulldown, cable crossover) and don't want to buy attachments separately.

## Fitness Reality 810XLT -- The Breakdown

<ProductCard productId="fitness-reality-810xlt" badge="Best Pure Rack" />

The 810XLT has been the default budget rack recommendation for years, and for good reason. At $329, it delivers 800 lbs of capacity with standard 2x2 hole spacing, which means tons of aftermarket attachments fit without modification. The multi-grip pull-up bar is solid, and the optional lat pulldown kit ($150 extra) covers most cable needs.

The trade-offs are fair at this price: 14-gauge steel is thinner than competitors, cables aren't included, and the plastic J-cup liners will eventually need replacing. None of these are dealbreakers for a home gym lifter.

## Sportsroyals Power Cage -- The Breakdown

<ProductCard productId="sportsroyals-power-cage" badge="Most Features" />

The Sportsroyals justifies its $549 price tag with sheer capability. A 1,600 lb weight capacity, 12-gauge steel, built-in cable crossover system, and more attachment points than you'll likely ever use. It's heavier and more stable out of the box than the 810XLT.

The downsides: the larger footprint demands more garage space, assembly runs 4+ hours even with a helper, and the cable weight stacks are light for anyone moving serious weight on pulldowns. At $549, it's still a bargain for what you get -- you'd pay $300-500 for an aftermarket cable crossover alone.

[Read our full Sportsroyals Power Cage review](/reviews/sportsroyals-power-cage-review/)

## The $220 Question

What does that extra $220 actually buy? Cables from day one, thicker steel (12-gauge vs 14-gauge), and double the rated weight capacity. That's a meaningful upgrade on paper.

But here's the math that matters: the 810XLT ($329) plus an aftermarket lat pulldown attachment ($150) totals $479 and gets you roughly 80% of the Sportsroyals' functionality for $70 less. You lose the cable crossover arms, but most home lifters rarely use those after the novelty wears off.

## Buy the 810XLT If...

- Budget is tight and every dollar counts
- You mainly squat, bench, and do pull-ups
- You want a simple, proven rack with years of community support
- You plan to add attachments later as your budget allows

## Buy the Sportsroyals If...

- You want cables on day one with zero add-on purchases
- You plan to do lat pulldowns, tricep pushdowns, and cable flyes regularly
- You want the heaviest-duty rack available under $600
- You have the space for a larger footprint

## What About the Mikolo F4?

The [Mikolo F4 at $489](/best-gear/mikolo-f4-vs-fitness-reality-810xlt/) splits the difference. It includes a LAT pulldown system (no cable crossover), 1,200 lb capacity, and 12-gauge steel. It's the middle ground if the 810XLT feels too bare and the Sportsroyals feels like overkill. See our [Mikolo vs Sportsroyals](/best-gear/mikolo-vs-sportsroyals/) comparison for that matchup, or our [three-way rack comparison](/best-gear/three-way-rack-comparison/) for the full picture.

## The Bottom Line

The Fitness Reality 810XLT remains the best pure power rack under $350. If you lift with a barbell and don't need cables, it's the move. The Sportsroyals is the better all-in-one home gym solution, but only if you'll actually use those cables enough to justify the premium. Most lifters are better off starting with the 810XLT and upgrading attachments over time.
