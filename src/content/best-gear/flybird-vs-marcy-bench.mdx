## FLYBIRD Adjustable Bench -- The Breakdown

<ProductCard productId="flybird-adjustable-bench" badge="Best Value" />

The FLYBIRD is the best-selling budget bench for a reason. At $109, you get 7 back positions, a 600 lb weight capacity, and a frame that folds nearly flat for storage. It works with any squat rack or power cage, making it one of the most versatile benches under $150.

**Strengths:** $109 price point, folds flat for tight spaces, 7 back angles (flat through military press), 600 lb rated capacity, pairs with any rack or stands.

**Weaknesses:** No built-in barbell catches. It is a standalone bench only -- you need a separate rack for barbell work.

Read our full [FLYBIRD Adjustable Bench review](/reviews/flybird-adjustable-bench-review/).

## Marcy Olympic Bench -- The Breakdown

<ProductCard productId="marcy-olympic-bench" badge="Best All-in-One" />

The Marcy Olympic is a complete bench press station in one piece. It comes with built-in barbell catches, a leg developer, a preacher curl pad, and Olympic plate storage posts. For lifters who want to bench press without buying a separate rack, this is the move.

**Strengths:** Built-in barbell catches for solo benching, leg developer attachment, preacher curl pad, integrated plate storage, complete station out of the box.

**Weaknesses:** $239 price tag, does not fold (needs dedicated floor space), fixed position makes it difficult to use inside a power rack, lower max capacity compared to the FLYBIRD for heavy lifters.

Read our full [Marcy Olympic Bench review](/reviews/marcy-olympic-bench-review/).

## Buy the FLYBIRD If...

- You already own a power rack or squat stands
- You want a bench that works for flat, incline, decline, and military press angles
- You need to fold and store the bench between sessions
- You are on a budget and want the best bench under $150
- You plan to upgrade your setup over time

## Buy the Marcy If...

- You do not have a rack and want an all-in-one bench press station
- You are a beginner who wants bench press, leg curls, and preacher curls in one piece of equipment
- You have dedicated gym space and do not need to fold anything away
- You want built-in plate storage to keep your gym organized

## The Bottom Line

For most home gym owners, the FLYBIRD at $109 is the smarter buy. It saves you $130, folds for storage, and pairs with any rack you own or plan to buy. The Marcy makes sense in one specific scenario: you want a standalone bench press station with accessories and you are not planning to buy a power rack.

If you are building a [home gym under $500](/builds/home-gym-under-500/), the FLYBIRD leaves more budget for plates, a rack, and other essentials. The Marcy eats nearly half that budget on the bench alone.
