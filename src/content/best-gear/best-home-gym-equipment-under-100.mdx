## Best Strength Equipment Under $100

<ProductCard productId="iron-gym-pull-up-bar" badge="Best Bodyweight Tool" />

The Iron Gym pull-up bar turns any doorframe into a full upper-body station. At $30, it's the single best investment you can make for your home gym. Read our [Iron Gym vs wall-mounted bar comparison](/best-gear/iron-gym-vs-wall-mounted-bar/) for a deeper look.

<ProductCard productId="cap-hex-dumbbells" badge="Best Fixed Dumbbells" />

CAP hex dumbbells are the workhorse of budget home gyms. Grab a pair of 20s or 25s and you've got enough load for curls, presses, rows, and lunges. See our [CAP hex vs Bowflex 552 comparison](/best-gear/cap-hex-vs-bowflex-552/) to decide between fixed and adjustable.

<ProductCard productId="bodylastics-resistance-bands" badge="Best Resistance Training" />

Bodylastics bands add variable resistance to any movement and pack flat for travel. They're a genuine training tool, not a rehab toy. Our [TRX vs Bodylastics breakdown](/best-gear/trx-vs-bodylastics/) covers how they compare to suspension trainers.

<ProductCard productId="yes4all-olympic-plates" badge="Best Weight Plates" />

If you already own a barbell, these plates let you load it without spending Rogue money. Solid cast iron, standard Olympic fit, and priced well under a dollar per pound.

<ProductCard productId="prosourcefit-dip-stand" badge="Best Dip Station" />

A dedicated dip stand opens up chest, tricep, and core work that's hard to replicate with other budget gear. At $90, it's the priciest item in this section but earns its spot. Check our [best power towers roundup](/best-gear/best-power-towers/) for heavier-duty alternatives.

## Best Core & Conditioning Under $100

<ProductCard productId="perfect-fitness-ab-wheel" badge="Best Core" />

The ab wheel is the most underrated core tool in existence. Five minutes of rollouts will humble anyone. At $27, there's no excuse not to own one.

<ProductCard productId="yes4all-slam-ball" badge="Best Conditioning" />

Slam balls turn any patch of floor into a conditioning station. Grab a 20 lb ball for $25 and pair it with burpees for a workout that rivals any cardio machine.

<ProductCard productId="rogue-style-weight-vest" badge="Best Weighted Training" />

A weight vest transforms bodyweight staples like push-ups, pull-ups, and walks into serious strength work. This is the piece that takes your training from "working out" to "training."

<ProductCard productId="wod-nation-jump-rope" badge="Best Cardio Accessory" />

At under $15, a speed rope is the cheapest effective cardio tool you can buy. Ten minutes of jump rope burns more calories than twenty minutes of jogging. See our [WOD Nation vs Crossrope comparison](/best-gear/wod-nation-vs-crossrope/) for a detailed look.

<ProductCard productId="stamina-compact-strider" badge="Best Budget Cardio" />

If jumping isn't your thing, the Stamina strider offers low-impact cardio that fits under a desk. It's not a Concept2, but at $80 it gets the job done. Browse our [best cardio machines roundup](/best-gear/best-cardio-machines-home-gym/) for more options.
