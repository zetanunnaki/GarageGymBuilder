## What Makes a Good Barbell

### Tensile Strength (PSI)
This measures how much force the bar can handle before permanently bending.

- **Under 150K PSI:** Budget bars. Will bend under heavy loads.
- **150K-190K PSI:** Mid-range. Good for most home gym lifters.
- **190K+ PSI:** Premium. Won't bend under any realistic load.

### Shaft Diameter
- **28mm:** Standard for Olympic weightlifting bars. Slightly whippier.
- **28.5mm:** The most common "power bar" diameter. Best all-around.
- **29mm:** Squat bars and specialty bars. Stiffer, no whip.

### Knurling
The crosshatch pattern cut into the shaft for grip.

- **Passive knurling:** Comfortable but slippery under heavy deadlifts
- **Moderate knurling:** The sweet spot for most lifters
- **Aggressive knurling:** Great grip, tears up hands during high-rep sets
- **Center knurling:** Needed for back squats. Some bars omit it.

### Sleeve Rotation
How freely the sleeves (ends where plates go) spin.

- **Bushings:** Affordable, adequate for powerlifting movements. Slower spin.
- **Needle bearings:** Smooth, fast spin. Required for Olympic lifts (cleans, snatches).

### Whip
How much the bar flexes under load.

- **Stiff bars:** Best for squats and bench. No energy lost to bar flex.
- **Whippy bars:** Best for Olympic lifts. The flex helps launch the bar during cleans.

## Bar Finish Guide

| Finish | Feel | Rust Resistance | Maintenance | Price Impact |
|--------|------|----------------|-------------|-------------|
| Bare Steel | Best grip | Lowest | Weekly oiling | Cheapest |
| Black Oxide | Good grip | Low | Regular oiling | Low |
| Zinc | Decent grip | Medium | Occasional | Medium |
| Cerakote | Smooth | High | Minimal | High |
| Chrome | Slick | High | Minimal | Medium |
| Stainless | Excellent grip | Highest | Almost none | Highest |

**Our recommendation for garage gyms:** Black oxide or zinc. Good grip, reasonable rust resistance, and won't break the bank. If your garage is humid, consider cerakote or stainless.

## Power Bar vs Olympic Bar vs All-Purpose

### Power Bar
- 29mm shaft, center knurling, stiff, bushing sleeves
- **Best for:** Squat, bench, deadlift focused training
- **Skip if:** You do Olympic lifts regularly

### Olympic Weightlifting Bar
- 28mm shaft, no center knurling, whippy, needle bearing sleeves
- **Best for:** Cleans, snatches, jerks
- **Skip if:** You only do powerlifting movements

### All-Purpose Bar
- 28.5mm shaft, center knurling, moderate whip, bushing sleeves
- **Best for:** Home gym lifters who do everything
- **This is what most people should buy**

## The Bottom Line
