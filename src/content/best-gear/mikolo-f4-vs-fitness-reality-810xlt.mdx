The Mikolo F4 ($489) vs Fitness Reality 810XLT ($329) is the most common budget power rack decision on Amazon. Both have thousands of 4.5+ star reviews. Both cost under $500. But they serve different lifters. Here's which one you should buy.

## The Quick Answer

**Buy the Fitness Reality 810XLT if:** You want the cheapest functional power rack and don't need a cable system. Proven with 5,000+ reviews.

**Buy the Mikolo F4 if:** You want a complete home gym setup with LAT pulldown and low row built-in. Worth the extra $160 for cable work.

## Build Quality

Both racks use 2x2" steel construction. Neither matches the 3x3" 11-gauge steel of premium racks, but both are sturdy for home gym use.

**Fitness Reality 810XLT:**
- 14-gauge steel (lighter)
- 800 lb weight capacity
- Simple, proven design
- Rattles slightly under heavy loads (normal for this price)

**Mikolo F4:**
- 12-gauge steel (heavier)
- 1,200 lb weight capacity
- More complex structure with cable system
- Rock-solid under heavy loads

**Winner: Mikolo F4** -- higher weight capacity and thicker steel.

## Features Included

This is the biggest differentiator between the two racks.

**Fitness Reality 810XLT Includes:**
- Power cage frame
- Pull-up bar
- Safety bars
- J-cups
- Weight plate storage pegs

**Mikolo F4 Includes:**
- Power cage frame
- Multi-grip pull-up bar
- Safety bars
- J-cups
- **LAT pulldown attachment**
- **Low row cable system**
- **Dual-track pulley system**
- **Landmine attachment**
- **Dip handles**
- Multiple attachment points

The Mikolo F4 is essentially a power rack + cable machine combo. The Fitness Reality is just a power rack.

**Winner: Mikolo F4** -- by a massive margin.

## Assembly

- **Fitness Reality 810XLT:** 90 minutes with 2 people
- **Mikolo F4:** 3-4 hours with 2 people

The Mikolo takes significantly longer because of the cable system complexity.

**Winner: Fitness Reality** -- faster setup.

## Weight Capacity

- **Fitness Reality 810XLT:** 800 lbs
- **Mikolo F4:** 1,200 lbs

For most home gym lifters, 800 lbs is more than enough. But if you're planning to grow into heavier squats and deadlifts, the Mikolo has more headroom.

**Winner: Mikolo F4**

## The Verdict

For most home gym builders, the **Mikolo F4** is the smarter purchase. The extra $160 gets you:

1. **LAT pulldown system** (saves $300 vs buying separately)
2. **Low row cable** (saves $150 vs buying separately)
3. **Higher weight capacity** (1,200 lb vs 800 lb)
4. **Thicker steel** (12-gauge vs 14-gauge)

Unless you're on a strict budget and absolutely can't afford $489, the Mikolo F4 delivers better value per dollar.

The Fitness Reality 810XLT remains an excellent choice for lifters who want the absolute cheapest functional rack and don't need cable work. Both are top-rated on Amazon and both will serve you well for years.
