The Yes4All Olympic Hex Trap Bar costs $130 on Amazon. The Rogue TB-2 costs $425 from Rogue Fitness directly. They both serve the same purpose: trap bar deadlifts, farmer's walks, and back-friendly pulling. Is the Rogue worth more than triple the price for home gym use? Here's the honest answer.

## The Quick Answer

**Buy the Yes4All if:** You want a great budget trap bar, you're not training competitively, or you want to keep all your purchases on Amazon.

**Buy the Rogue TB-2 if:** You demand premium quality, you compete or train at elite levels, or you value made-in-USA manufacturing.

## Build Quality

**Yes4All:** Solid welded steel construction. Powder coat finish. Functional and durable.
**Rogue TB-2:** Premium American steel. Black zinc finish. Heirloom-quality build.

The Rogue is objectively better-built. The welds are cleaner, the steel is higher grade, and the finish is more durable. The Yes4All is good, but it's not Rogue good.

That said, "good enough" is plenty for home gym use. The Yes4All will last 10+ years of normal use without issues.

**Winner: Rogue** (objectively)

## Knurling

**Yes4All:** Light knurling — chalk recommended for max-effort pulls.
**Rogue:** Medium-aggressive knurling — grippy without being painful.

Knurling matters more on a trap bar than most people realize. With 500+ lbs in your hands, light knurling can slip. The Rogue gives you confident grip without chalk.

For most home lifters pulling under 400 lbs, the Yes4All knurling is fine. For heavy pullers, the Rogue is noticeably better.

**Winner: Rogue**

## Weight Capacity

**Yes4All:** 750 lbs rated.
**Rogue TB-2:** 1,000+ lbs rated.

Both exceed what 99% of home lifters will ever pull. Unless you're a competitive strongman or elite powerlifter, the Yes4All capacity is more than enough.

**Winner: Tie** (for practical purposes)

## Availability

**Yes4All:** Amazon Prime, Walmart, ships in 2 days.
**Rogue:** Rogue Fitness website only. Custom orders, 1-3 week shipping.

This is a major practical difference. The Yes4All shows up at your door in 2 days from Amazon Prime. Rogue takes weeks and ships from a single warehouse in Ohio.

**Winner: Yes4All** (massively)

## Resale Value

**Yes4All:** Limited resale market. Sells for ~50% of new on Facebook Marketplace.
**Rogue:** Strong resale market. Sells for ~75% of new used.

If you ever sell your gym, Rogue gear retains value much better. Yes4All gear loses value faster.

**Winner: Rogue**

## Cost-Per-Year Math

**Yes4All:** $130 / 10 years = **$13/year**
**Rogue TB-2:** $425 / 30+ years = **$14/year**

When amortized over realistic lifespans, the cost-per-year is nearly identical. The Rogue's premium pricing buys you more years, not more value per year.
