If you can do 10+ bodyweight pull-ups or 15+ bodyweight dips, you've outgrown bodyweight training. The next progression is adding weight, and a dip belt is the cleanest way to do it. Here are our top picks after months of testing.

## Why You Need a Dip Belt

Holding a dumbbell between your feet stops working past about 30 lbs. A dip belt:

- Loads up to 200+ lbs comfortably
- Frees your legs to move naturally
- Works for pull-ups, dips, AND belt squats
- Costs less than a pair of nice shoes

For anyone past beginner strength, a dip belt is the cheapest, most effective progression tool you can buy.

## What to Look For

### 1. Padding Width
Wider padding = more comfort under heavy loads. Look for 4-6 inches of neoprene padding minimum.

### 2. Chain Length
Most chains are 30-36 inches. Tall users (6'2"+) should look for the longer end.

### 3. Weight Capacity
Most belts are rated 200-300 lbs. For most users, 200 lbs is plenty. Strong lifters want 250+.

### 4. Material
Neoprene is comfortable but absorbs sweat. Leather is durable but stiff. Nylon is the middle ground.

### 5. Carabiner/Buckle
Steel carabiners are bulletproof. Plastic buckles work but feel cheap. Some belts have neither — the chain attaches directly to a D-ring.

## When to Add Weight

A general progression for weighted pull-ups:

- **Bodyweight x 10 reps:** Add 10 lbs
- **+10 lbs x 8 reps:** Add 5 lbs
- **+25 lbs x 5 reps:** Add 5 lbs
- **+45 lbs x 5 reps:** Stronger than 95% of the population

Same logic applies to dips. Don't try to add weight before you can do 10 clean bodyweight reps — you'll just compensate with bad form.

## Beyond Pull-Ups and Dips

The dip belt also enables:

### Belt Squats
Stand on two boxes, hang weight from the belt between your feet, squat down. Trains legs without spinal compression — a game-changer for anyone with back issues.

### Weighted Push-Ups
Loop the chain over your back with a plate attached. Works at moderate loads only.

### Weighted Inverted Rows
Hang the weight from your hips during rows for added back stimulus.
