A rowing machine is the single best cardio investment for a home gym. It's full-body, low-impact, and burns more calories per minute than almost any other machine. The hard part is choosing one — there are four resistance types and prices ranging from $200 to $2,000+.

This guide breaks down the four types and our top picks at every budget.

## The Four Types of Rowing Resistance

### 1. Magnetic Resistance
- **How it works:** Magnets create resistance against a flywheel
- **Pros:** Quiet, smooth, manual resistance settings
- **Cons:** Lower max resistance, less competitive feel
- **Best for:** Apartments, early morning training, general fitness

### 2. Air Resistance
- **How it works:** A fan creates resistance — pull harder, more resistance
- **Pros:** Unlimited resistance, competition standard (Concept2)
- **Cons:** Loud, no manual settings
- **Best for:** Serious training, competition, CrossFit

### 3. Water Resistance
- **How it works:** Paddles spin in a water tank
- **Pros:** Smooth feel, beautiful sound, unlimited resistance
- **Cons:** Expensive, heavy, requires water maintenance
- **Best for:** Aesthetic-conscious users, those who hate machine noise

### 4. Hydraulic Resistance
- **How it works:** Piston-based resistance like a car shock
- **Pros:** Cheap, compact
- **Cons:** Inconsistent feel, breaks down over time
- **Best for:** Budget shoppers, casual users (skip if possible)

## What to Look For

### 1. Length
Most rowers are 80-95 inches long. Measure your space carefully — a rower needs ~9 feet of clearance to use comfortably.

### 2. Storage
Vertical fold (Sunny, Concept2) saves significant floor space. Non-folding rowers need a dedicated spot.

### 3. Weight Capacity
Most home rowers handle 250-300 lbs user weight. Heavier users should look for 350+ lb rated models.

### 4. Console Features
Basic: time, distance, calories, stroke rate. Premium: heart rate, programs, app connectivity. For most users, basic is enough.

### 5. Seat Comfort
The biggest comfort issue on long rows is the seat. Test in person if possible, or buy a $20 gel cover for unpadded seats.

## Programming a Rower

### Beginner Workout (15 min)
- 5 min easy warm-up
- 5 x 1 min hard / 1 min easy
- 5 min cool down

### Intermediate Workout (25 min)
- 5 min easy warm-up
- 4 x 500m intervals at 80% effort, 1 min rest
- 5 min cool down

### Advanced Workout (45 min)
- 10 min easy warm-up
- 30 min steady-state at 70% max heart rate
- 5 min cool down
