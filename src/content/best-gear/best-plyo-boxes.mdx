A plyo box is one of those purchases you don't think you need until you have one. Box jumps for power, step-ups for legs, decline push-ups for shoulders, dips between two boxes — it's a Swiss Army knife of conditioning. Here are our top picks.

## Three Types of Plyo Boxes

### 1. Wooden Plyo Boxes
- **Material:** 13-ply birch or similar plywood
- **Pros:** Sturdy, looks great, supports all exercises
- **Cons:** Sharp edges shred shins on missed jumps
- **Best for:** Confident jumpers, multi-purpose use

### 2. Foam Plyo Boxes
- **Material:** Dense foam with vinyl cover
- **Pros:** Forgiving on missed jumps (no shin shredding)
- **Cons:** Compresses over time, less stable for step-ups
- **Best for:** Beginners learning box jumps, anxious jumpers

### 3. Steel Plyo Boxes
- **Material:** Welded steel frame
- **Pros:** Indestructible, professional look
- **Cons:** Most dangerous on missed jumps, cold to touch
- **Best for:** Commercial gyms, hardcore CrossFitters

For most home gyms, wooden is the right answer. Foam is the safe choice for beginners.

## Box Jump Safety

Box jumps are deceptively dangerous. Follow these rules:

1. **Start lower than you think** — pride causes injuries
2. **Land softly** — quiet landings = good landings
3. **Step down, don't jump down** — landing impact ruins knees
4. **Check the box** — make sure it doesn't slide on your floor
5. **Sand sharp edges** — wooden boxes need this on day one

## Beyond Box Jumps

A plyo box does way more than jumps:

### Step-Ups
The most underrated leg exercise. Hold dumbbells, step up onto the box, drive through the heel. Brutal effective.

### Bulgarian Split Squats
Rear foot elevated on box, front foot on floor. Build single-leg strength.

### Box Squats
Squat down to the box, sit briefly, drive up. Powerlifting accessory and form-builder.

### Decline Push-Ups
Feet elevated on box, hands on floor. More chest and shoulder activation.

### Dips
Position two boxes parallel, dip between them. Free chest and tricep development.
