The Mikolo F4 2.0 ($489) vs Sportsroyals Power Cage ($549) is the most relevant power rack comparison on Amazon in 2026. Both include cable systems, both handle 1,000+ lbs, and both are top-rated. So which one should you buy?

We tested both extensively. Here's our definitive comparison.

## The Quick Answer

**Buy the Mikolo F4 if:** You want excellent value with a LAT pulldown at the lowest price. Proven with 3,000+ Amazon reviews.

**Buy the Sportsroyals if:** You want maximum features including a full cable crossover system. Higher weight capacity (1,600 lbs) and more attachments included.

## Build Quality

Both racks use similar 2x2" steel construction. At their price points, neither uses the premium 3x3" 11-gauge steel of direct-from-manufacturer racks, but both are perfectly sturdy for home gym use.

**Mikolo F4:** 12-gauge steel, clean welds, solid construction. The pulley system is smooth out of the box.

**Sportsroyals:** Similar steel gauge, slightly heavier overall. The cable crossover adds significant structural complexity and weight.

**Winner: Tie** — Both are well-built for their price class.

## Cable System

This is where the biggest differences appear.

**Mikolo F4:**
- LAT pulldown with dual-track pulley
- Low row cable
- Single cable weight loading point
- Smooth operation

**Sportsroyals:**
- Full adjustable cable crossover (dual arms)
- LAT pulldown
- Low row
- Multiple cable attachment points
- More exercise variety

<PriceAlert productId="sportsroyals-power-cage" note="Includes a full cable crossover system that adds $300-500 in value vs aftermarket." />

**Winner: Sportsroyals** — More cable options and versatility.

## Weight Capacity

- **Mikolo F4:** 1,200 lbs rated
- **Sportsroyals:** 1,600 lbs rated

Both are overbuilt for home gym use. Unless you're squatting 700+ lbs, either is fine.

**Winner: Sportsroyals** — by a significant margin on paper.

## Price & Value

## Assembly

- **Mikolo F4:** 3-4 hours with two people
- **Sportsroyals:** 4-5 hours with two people

Both require patience. The Sportsroyals takes longer due to the cable crossover complexity.

**Winner: Mikolo** — slightly faster assembly.

## Amazon Reviews

- **Mikolo F4:** 4.6+ stars, 3,000+ reviews
- **Sportsroyals:** 4.7+ stars, fewer reviews (newer product)

Both have excellent ratings, but the Mikolo has more review volume.

**Winner: Mikolo** — longer proven track record.

## Our Pick

For most home gym builders, the **Mikolo F4** is the smarter purchase. You save $60, get the proven product with more reviews, and the LAT pulldown covers 90% of what cable crossover training offers. Unless you specifically need the crossover movements (cable flyes, face pulls, woodchoppers), the Mikolo is the better value.

That said, if you have the budget and want the complete home gym experience, the Sportsroyals is genuinely one of the best values in the market. You'd need to spend 2-3x as much to get similar functionality elsewhere.
