The big purchases get all the attention — racks, barbells, plates. But the small accessories under $50 often have the biggest impact on your daily training experience. Here are the ones we actually use every session.

## The Essential Accessories

### 1. Lifting Chalk ($8-15)
The single best training accessory in existence. Liquid or block, it transforms your grip on deadlifts, pull-ups, and rows. Every gym should have chalk.

### 2. Barbell Collars ($15-30)
Spring clips work, but they slide. Invest in a pair of Lock-Jaw or similar clamp collars. They hold plates securely and take 2 seconds to change.

### 3. Resistance Bands ($20-40 for a set)
Versatile beyond belief. Warm-up, mobility, banded exercises, assisted pull-ups, and accommodating resistance for squats and bench. Buy a variety pack with light through heavy.

### 4. Foam Roller ($15-25)
Recovery isn't optional. A basic foam roller handles quads, IT band, thoracic spine, and lats. Use it before and after every session.

### 5. Jump Rope ($10-20)
The cheapest cardio equipment in existence. 10 minutes of jump rope is a legitimate conditioning session. Speed ropes are fast and portable.

### 6. Wrist Wraps ($12-20)
Essential for overhead pressing and heavy bench. They stabilize the wrist joint and let you focus on the lift instead of wrist pain. See our [Rip Toned Wrist Wraps review](/reviews/rip-toned-wrist-wraps-review/) for our top pick.

### 7. Knee Sleeves ($30-50/pair)
Neoprene knee sleeves provide compression, warmth, and proprioceptive feedback during squats. They don't add strength — they keep your knees healthy.

### 8. Ab Wheel ($12-20)
The best core exercise tool for the money. Ab wheel rollouts are brutally effective and take up zero space when stored.

### 9. Fractional Plates ($25-40)
0.5 lb and 1 lb change plates allow micro-progression on overhead press and bench when 5 lb jumps become too large. Critical for intermediate lifters.

### 10. Gym Timer ($20-40)
A wall-mounted interval timer for rest periods, Tabata, and EMOM workouts. Better than using your phone (no distractions).
