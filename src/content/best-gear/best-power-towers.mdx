A power tower is the most underrated piece of home gym equipment. For $90-150, you get a station that handles four fundamental bodyweight exercises: pull-ups, dips, leg raises, and push-ups. No wall mounting, no drilling, no installation — just assemble and train.

We bought and tested four of Amazon's top-selling power towers. Here's how they stack up.

## Who Should Buy What

**Buy the Sportsroyals if:** You want the most stable, durable tower and plan to add weighted dips/pull-ups over time.

**Buy the RELIFE if:** You want good quality at a fair price and train with strict form.

**Buy the Stamina if:** You're under 200 lbs, on a tight budget, and just need basic bodyweight stations.

**Buy the ProsourceFit if:** You only need dips and want to save space.

## Do You Even Need a Power Tower?

If you already have a power rack with a pull-up bar and dip attachment, probably not. But if you train in a space without a rack — apartment, office, outdoor patio — a power tower gives you the four most important bodyweight exercises in one compact station.

For $150 or less, it's one of the best value-per-exercise investments in home fitness.
