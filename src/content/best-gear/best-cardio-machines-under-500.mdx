## Cardio Machine Buyer's Guide

Before you buy, weigh these four factors:

1. **Noise** -- Magnetic resistance (rowers, spin bikes) is nearly silent. Belt-driven treadmills produce moderate noise. If you train early or late, noise should be your top filter.
2. **Footprint** -- Measure your available space first. Folding machines like the Sunny rower and XTERRA treadmill reclaim floor space when stored. The Stamina strider needs almost none.
3. **Resistance type** -- Air resistance scales infinitely but is loud. Magnetic resistance is quiet and consistent but caps out at a fixed max. Friction resistance (some spin bikes) wears down over time.
4. **Maintenance** -- Budget machines need more upkeep. Lubricate treadmill belts every 3 months, check spin bike brake pads, and tighten rower rail bolts quarterly. Neglect these and a $250 machine becomes a $0 clothes rack.
