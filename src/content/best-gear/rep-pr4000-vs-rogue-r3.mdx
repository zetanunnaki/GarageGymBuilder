The REP PR-4000 ($649) vs Rogue R-3 ($850) is the most common power rack comparison in the home gym world. Both are 3x3" 11-gauge steel racks with massive attachment ecosystems. Both will last forever. So which one deserves your money?

We've trained on both racks for months. Here's our definitive comparison.

## The Quick Answer

**Buy the REP PR-4000 if:** You want the best value and don't care about brand prestige. You get 90% of the Rogue experience for 75% of the price.

**Buy the Rogue R-3 if:** You value American manufacturing, want the largest attachment ecosystem, and plan to hold the rack for 20+ years.

## Build Quality

Both racks use 3x3" 11-gauge steel. In terms of raw structural integrity, they're nearly identical. The difference is in the finish and attention to detail.

**Rogue:** Thicker powder coat, cleaner welds, tighter hole tolerances. Everything feels precision-machined. The Westside hole spacing is laser-cut with numbered markings.

**REP:** Very good finish, occasionally minor cosmetic imperfections. Numbered uprights. The steel itself is identical quality — the cosmetic differences are minor.

**Winner: Rogue** — by a small margin. Both are excellent.

## Attachment Ecosystem

This is where the comparison gets interesting.

**Rogue Monster Lite:** 50+ attachments available. Matador dip handle, spotter arms, landmine, j-cups, band pegs, cable systems, and more. Third-party compatibility is massive.

**REP PR-4000:** 30+ attachments. Growing rapidly. Dip attachment, lat pulldown, j-cups, spotter arms, and more. Some unique options like the ISO arms.

**Winner: Rogue** — larger ecosystem, more third-party options. But REP is closing the gap.

**Winner: REP** — significantly better value.

## Resale Value

If you ever sell your rack:

- **Rogue** holds 80-90% of retail value on the used market
- **REP** holds 60-70% of retail value

Rogue's brand recognition commands a premium on the secondary market.

**Winner: Rogue** — by a significant margin.
