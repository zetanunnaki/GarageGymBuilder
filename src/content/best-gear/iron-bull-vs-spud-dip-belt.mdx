If you're past beginner strength on pull-ups and dips, you need a dip belt. The Iron Bull Strength Dip Belt is the best-selling dip belt on Amazon at $50. The Spud Inc Deluxe Dip Belt is the powerlifting cult favorite at $85. Which should you actually buy? Here's the honest breakdown.

## The Quick Answer

**Buy the Iron Bull if:** You're loading under 200 lbs added, you're new to weighted calisthenics, or you want the best Amazon-available dip belt.

**Buy the Spud Inc if:** You're loading over 250 lbs added, you want a strap (not chain) design, or you're a powerlifter using it for belt squats.

## Head-to-Head Comparison

| Feature | Iron Bull Strength | Spud Inc Deluxe |
|---------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Price | $50 | $85 |
| Available On | Amazon, Walmart | Spud Inc Direct |
| Weight Capacity | 270 lbs added | 600+ lbs added |
| Padding | Wide neoprene | Wider nylon |
| Connection | Steel chain | Heavy nylon strap |
| Warranty | Limited | Lifetime |
| Country | Imported | USA |

## Padding Comfort

**Iron Bull:** Wide neoprene padding distributes weight comfortably for most users.
**Spud Inc:** Even wider nylon padding (5+ inches), specifically designed for ultra-heavy loads.

Under 200 lbs added load, both are comfortable. Above 200 lbs, the Spud Inc's wider padding is noticeably more comfortable. Above 300 lbs, the Iron Bull becomes painful while the Spud stays manageable.

**Winner: Spud Inc** (for heavy loads)

## Connection Type

**Iron Bull:** Steel chain with carabiner. Adjustable length.
**Spud Inc:** Heavy nylon strap. No metal contact.

This is a major difference. Chains can feel cold against your legs in winter, can pinch skin if positioned wrong, and add metal weight to the system. Straps are warmer, more flexible, and lighter.

For belt squats specifically, the Spud Inc strap design is superior because it doesn't dig into your inner thighs like chain links do.

**Winner: Spud Inc** (for comfort)

## Maximum Weight Capacity

**Iron Bull:** 270 lbs added load (manufacturer rated).
**Spud Inc:** 600+ lbs added load.

For 95% of users, 270 lbs added is more than enough. Most lifters never add more than 100 lbs to pull-ups or dips. The Spud Inc capacity is overkill for everyone except elite strength athletes.

**Winner: Tie** (for practical use)

## Belt Squat Use

If you want to use a dip belt for belt squats (squatting with weight hanging from your hips), the Spud Inc is significantly better. The strap design is wider, more comfortable, and doesn't dig into your inner thighs during the squat motion.

The Iron Bull works for occasional belt squats but isn't ideal as a dedicated belt squat tool.

**Winner: Spud Inc** (for belt squats)

## Availability and Shipping

**Iron Bull:** Amazon Prime, ships in 2 days.
**Spud Inc:** Spud Inc website only. Ships from Tennessee in 1-2 weeks.

The Iron Bull's Amazon Prime availability is a major practical advantage. Need a dip belt by the weekend? Iron Bull is in stock. Spud Inc requires planning ahead.

**Winner: Iron Bull** (massively)

## Long-Term Durability

**Iron Bull:** Will last 5-10 years of normal use. Neoprene compresses over time.
**Spud Inc:** Built to last 20+ years. Strap design has no degradation issues.

The Spud Inc is bulletproof — you can buy a 15-year-old one used and it'll still work perfectly. The Iron Bull is well-built but won't last forever.

**Winner: Spud Inc**

**Iron Bull:** $50 / 7 years = **~$7/year**

The Spud Inc is technically cheaper per year because it lasts longer. But you're paying $35 more upfront for a benefit that takes 7+ years to realize.

## Who Should Buy Which

**Buy the Iron Bull if you:**
- Are loading under 200 lbs added
- Want fast Amazon Prime shipping
- Are new to weighted calisthenics
- Want the cheapest legit option
- Don't need belt squats

**Buy the Spud Inc if you:**
- Are loading over 250 lbs added
- Use the belt for belt squats
- Want made-in-USA manufacturing
- Plan to keep it forever
- Hate chain digging into your legs

## Final Verdict

The Spud Inc is the better dip belt — wider padding, better connection design, longer lifespan, made in USA. There's a reason it's the powerlifting community favorite.

But "better" doesn't always mean "smarter purchase." For **most home gym owners** who'll never load past 200 lbs, the Iron Bull is the smarter buy. It saves $35, ships from Amazon Prime, and does everything you actually need.

Upgrade to the Spud Inc only if you outgrow the Iron Bull or specifically need it for heavy belt squats.
