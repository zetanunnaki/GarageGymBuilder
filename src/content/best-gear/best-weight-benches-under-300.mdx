## What to Look For in a Budget Bench

1. **Weight Capacity** — Aim for 600 lbs minimum. Add your bodyweight plus the barbell load, then factor in dynamic force. Cheap benches rated at 300 lbs are a liability.
2. **Pad Angle Range** — Flat-only benches are the most stable but limit exercise variety. Adjustable benches with at least 5-6 positions (decline through 80-85 degrees) cover nearly every pressing angle.
3. **Stability** — The bench should not wobble, shift, or flex during heavy pressing. Test with heavy dumbbell presses — that's where instability reveals itself.
4. **Foldability** — If your gym doubles as a garage or living space, a bench that folds to 20-30% of its footprint is a real advantage. The FLYBIRD nails this; the Marcy does not.
