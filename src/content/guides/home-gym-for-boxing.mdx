Boxing is one of the best total-body workouts ever invented. Cardiovascular conditioning, full-body power, mental toughness, footwork, hand-eye coordination — all in 30 minutes a day. A home boxing gym lets you train daily without commercial gym hassles.

This guide covers exactly what you need.

## The Three Tiers of Boxing Training

### Tier 1: Fitness Boxer (90% of People)
- Wants the workout, not the fights
- Trains 3-5 days per week
- Needs: heavy bag, gloves, jump rope, hand wraps
- Total cost: $200-400

### Tier 2: Skill Boxer
- Wants to learn real technique
- Trains 4-6 days per week
- Adds: double-end bag, speed bag, mirror
- Total cost: $400-800

### Tier 3: Competitive Fighter
- Wants to compete (amateur or pro)
- Trains 6 days per week
- Adds: ring time at a real gym (you can't sparring train alone)
- Total cost: $800+ at home, plus gym membership

For 90% of readers, Tier 1 is the right starting point.

## Essential Equipment

### 1. Heavy Bag ($100-200)
The single most important purchase. Look for:
- 70-100 lbs minimum (lighter bags swing too much)
- Hanging or freestanding (hanging is better for power, freestanding for apartments)
- Filled with sand/cloth (not water — water bags slosh)

### 2. Boxing Gloves ($40-80)
- **Bag work:** 12-14 oz (smaller, more wrist support)
- **Sparring:** 16 oz (more padding for safety)
- For most fitness boxers, one pair of 14 oz gloves does everything

### 3. Hand Wraps ($10)
Non-negotiable. Wraps protect your wrists and knuckles from injury. Buy 180-inch Mexican-style wraps. Two pairs minimum.

## Sample Boxing Workout (45 minutes)

### Warm-Up (10 min)
- 3 rounds jump rope, 1 minute on / 30 sec off
- Shadow boxing 2 rounds, 3 min each
- Dynamic stretching

### Main Work (25 min)
- **Heavy bag rounds:** 5 x 3 min rounds, 1 min rest
  - Round 1: Jab, jab-cross combinations
  - Round 2: Jab-cross-hook combinations
  - Round 3: Body shots (hooks, uppercuts)
  - Round 4: Defensive movement + counters
  - Round 5: All-out conditioning round

### Conditioning (10 min)
- Burpees, push-ups, sit-ups, jumping jacks
- 1 min on / 30 sec off
- Total 7 rounds

## Training Tips for Beginners

1. **Watch tutorials** — YouTube has world-class boxing instruction for free. Start with Tony Jeffries channel.

2. **Master the jab first** — 80% of boxing is the jab. Spend hours just on jabs before adding combinations.

3. **Footwork is everything** — punch power comes from feet, not arms. Practice footwork drills daily.

4. **Hit with the first two knuckles** — index and middle. Hitting with the pinky knuckles breaks hands.

5. **Always wear hand wraps** — even for light bag work. Wrist injuries take months to heal.

## Setting Up the Heavy Bag

### Hanging Setup
- Mount to a ceiling joist (not drywall)
- Use a swivel mount to prevent rope twisting
- Allow 8 feet of clearance around the bag
- Floor space needed: ~8 ft x 8 ft

### Freestanding Setup
- Use base filled with sand (water sloshes)
- Place on rubber matting to prevent sliding
- Floor space needed: ~6 ft x 6 ft
