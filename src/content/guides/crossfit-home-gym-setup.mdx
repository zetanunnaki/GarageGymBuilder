CrossFit in a home gym is not only possible — it's better than most CrossFit gyms. No class schedules, no waiting for equipment, no scaling because of space limits. With the right setup, you can do every Hero WOD and benchmark in your garage.

This guide covers exactly what to buy and how to spend your budget.

## What CrossFit Actually Needs

Before we dive into equipment, let's clarify what CrossFit-style training requires:

**Strength (required):**
- Power rack or squat stands
- Olympic barbell
- Bumper plates (for drops) OR cast iron + platform
- Pull-up bar

**Conditioning (required):**
- Air bike OR rower OR jump rope
- Flat surface for burpees/running
- Kettlebells

**Accessories (helpful):**
- Dumbbells
- Wall ball (optional — sandbag works)
- Rowing machine (if budget allows)
- Gymnastics rings

## Sample CrossFit Home Gym Workouts

### "Fran" (Benchmark WOD)
21-15-9 reps for time:
- Thruster (95/65 lbs)
- Pull-Ups

**Equipment needed:** Barbell, plates, pull-up bar.

### "Helen" (Benchmark WOD)
3 rounds for time:
- 400m run
- 21 KB Swings (53/35 lbs)
- 12 Pull-Ups

**Equipment needed:** 53 lb kettlebell, pull-up bar, space for running.

### Air Bike Intervals
10 rounds:
- 30 seconds max effort on air bike
- 30 seconds rest

**Equipment needed:** Air bike, timer.

### DT (Hero WOD)
5 rounds for time:
- 12 Deadlifts (155/105)
- 9 Hang Power Cleans (155/105)
- 6 Push Jerks (155/105)

**Equipment needed:** Barbell, bumper plates, space to drop barbell safely.

## What to Skip

Don't waste money on:

- **Wall balls** — use a sandbag or heavy dumbbell
- **GHD machines** — expensive, use bodyweight alternatives
- **Slam balls** — use a jump rope or kettlebell instead
- **Plyo boxes** — DIY from plywood for $30
- **Rogue anything at first** — Amazon alternatives work fine
