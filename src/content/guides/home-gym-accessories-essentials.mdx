Walk into any garage gym that's been running for 5+ years and you'll notice the same accessories on every wall, shelf, and hook. Not the flashy gadgets from Instagram ads — the boring, functional tools that serious lifters use every single session.

Here are the 15 that matter, ranked by priority. If you're building a gym from scratch, buy them in this order.

Spring clips are fine until they're not. The first time a plate slides mid-squat, you'll wish you had real collars. Aluminum quick-release collars lock instantly and won't budge under heavy loads.

### 2. Lifting Belt

<ProductCard productId="dark-iron-lifting-belt" />

A leather belt increases intra-abdominal pressure, which stabilizes your spine under heavy squats and deadlifts. This isn't optional equipment — it's safety equipment. Every lifter squatting over 225 lbs should own one.

### 3. Chalk or Liquid Chalk

<ProductCard productId="liquid-grip-chalk" />

Sweaty hands are a limiting factor on deadlifts, pull-ups, and rows. Chalk solves it instantly. Liquid chalk is cleaner than block chalk and gym-friendly — no dust on your equipment.

### 4. Gym Flooring

<ProductCard productId="balancefrom-puzzle-mat" />

Protects your floor, deadens sound, prevents equipment from sliding, and saves your knees on standing exercises. EVA puzzle mats are the cheapest option that actually works.

## What NOT to Buy

- **Ab machines** — Use your barbell, floor, and pull-up bar instead
- **Ankle weights for walking** — Do farmer walks with dumbbells instead
- **Vibration plates** — No evidence they build muscle or burn meaningful calories
- **Smith machine attachments for power racks** — Get a real barbell instead
- **Shake weights** — Just... no
