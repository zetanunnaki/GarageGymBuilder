MMA and combat sports require a different training focus than traditional strength training. You need explosive power, unmatched conditioning, strength endurance, and functional movement — all of which can be built in a well-equipped home gym.

This guide covers exactly what fighters need for garage gym training.

## What Combat Sports Actually Require

Fighters need:
- **Strength** — enough to dominate in clinches and grappling
- **Explosive power** — for striking, takedowns, transitions
- **Conditioning** — to maintain pace over multiple rounds
- **Grip strength** — for grappling and clinch work
- **Core strength** — the foundation for all punches, kicks, and transitions
- **Mobility** — to throw high kicks, shoot takedowns, and scramble

A pure powerlifting gym won't give you all of this. Let's build something better.

## Additional Fight-Specific Equipment

Beyond standard gym equipment, fighters benefit from:

### Heavy Bag (Essential for Striking)
- **Cost:** $100-250
- **Why:** Practice strikes without a training partner
- **Setup:** Ceiling mount or freestanding

### Boxing Gloves & Hand Wraps
- **Cost:** $50-150
- **Why:** Required for bag work
- **Notes:** Get 12-14 oz gloves for training, 16 oz for hard sparring

### Jump Rope (Cheapest, Most Valuable)
- **Cost:** $10-30
- **Why:** Footwork, conditioning, warm-up
- **Notes:** Speed rope or weighted rope both work

### Gymnastics Rings
- **Cost:** $30-50
- **Why:** Upper body strength, core stability, grip
- **Setup:** Hang from pull-up bar

### Medicine Ball (Optional)
- **Cost:** $40-80
- **Why:** Rotational power work (slams, throws)
- **Notes:** 10-20 lbs is plenty for most fighters

## Sample Fighter's Training Week

### Monday: Strength (Lower)
- Back Squat: 5x5
- Trap Bar Deadlift: 3x5
- Bulgarian Split Squat: 3x8 each leg
- Plank: 3x30 sec

### Tuesday: Conditioning + Skill
- 5 min jump rope warm-up
- 10 rounds of 30 sec hard / 30 sec easy on Assault Bike
- 30 min bag work or skill practice

### Wednesday: Strength (Upper)
- Bench Press: 5x5
- Weighted Pull-Ups: 4x5
- Overhead Press: 3x8
- Dumbbell Row: 3x10
- Farmer's Walk: 3x30 steps

### Thursday: Active Recovery
- 30 min easy walk or light bike
- Stretching / mobility
- Foam rolling

### Friday: Conditioning + Explosive Power
- Kettlebell Swings: 10x10 (EMOM format)
- Box Jumps: 5x5
- Power Clean: 5x3
- Plyometric push-ups: 3x10

### Saturday: Skill + Sparring
- Technique practice
- Light sparring or grappling
- Heavy bag rounds

### Sunday: Rest
