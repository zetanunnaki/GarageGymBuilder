You can have the best equipment in the world, but without proper nutrition, you're leaving gains on the table. The good news: gym nutrition isn't complicated. Here's what actually matters.

## The Three Fundamentals

### 1. Protein: The Non-Negotiable
Protein builds and repairs muscle. Without enough, your training is partially wasted.

**How much:** 0.7-1.0 grams per pound of bodyweight per day.
- 150 lb person: 105-150g protein daily
- 200 lb person: 140-200g protein daily

**Best sources:** Chicken breast, ground turkey, eggs, Greek yogurt, whey protein, fish, lean beef.

**Timing:** Spread protein across 3-5 meals. Post-workout protein within 2 hours of training is beneficial but not magical.

### 2. Calories: The Lever
Your calorie intake determines whether you gain, lose, or maintain weight.

| Goal | Calorie Target |
|------|---------------|
| Lose fat | 300-500 calories below maintenance |
| Maintain | Eat at maintenance (TDEE) |
| Build muscle | 200-300 calories above maintenance |

**How to find your maintenance:** Multiply your bodyweight by 14-16. A 180 lb person maintains at roughly 2,500-2,900 calories.

### 3. Consistency: The Multiplier
The best diet is the one you can sustain. Don't overthink macros, meal timing, or supplements until you've been consistent with protein and calories for 3+ months.

## Supplements That Actually Work

### Tier 1: Evidence-Based (Worth Buying)
- **Creatine Monohydrate** ($15/month) — the most researched supplement in history. 5g daily. Improves strength, power, and recovery.
- **Whey Protein** ($30-50/month) — convenient way to hit protein targets. Not magic, just food.
- **Caffeine** ($0-10/month) — improves training performance. Coffee counts.

### Tier 2: Helpful for Some
- **Vitamin D** ($10/month) — if you train in a garage and don't get much sun
- **Fish Oil** ($15/month) — joint health benefits, especially for older lifters
- **Magnesium** ($10/month) — sleep quality improvement for some people

### Tier 3: Skip These (Save Your Money)
- **BCAAs** — unnecessary if you eat adequate protein
- **Pre-workout** — caffeine is the only ingredient that matters. Buy coffee.
- **Mass gainers** — just expensive calories. Eat real food instead.
- **Testosterone boosters** — none of them work. Save your money.
- **Fat burners** — the "burn" is from caffeine. Buy coffee.

## Pre-Workout Nutrition

**1-2 hours before training:** A balanced meal with protein and carbs.
- Example: Chicken and rice, oatmeal with protein powder, or a sandwich

**30 minutes before (if you train early):** Something light.
- Example: Banana, rice cake with peanut butter, or a protein shake

**Fasted training:** Fine if you prefer it. Performance may suffer slightly but fat loss is not affected.

## Post-Workout Nutrition

**Within 2 hours:** A meal with protein and carbs.
- Example: Protein shake with fruit, chicken and potatoes, or eggs and toast

**The "anabolic window" myth:** You don't need to slam a shake within 30 seconds of your last rep. Eat a normal meal within a couple hours.

## Hydration

- **During training:** 16-24 oz of water per hour of training
- **Daily total:** Half your bodyweight in ounces (minimum)
- **Signs of dehydration:** Dark urine, headache, reduced performance, dizziness

## The Bottom Line

Eat enough protein (0.7-1.0g per pound), manage your calories based on your goal, and be consistent. Take creatine. Everything else is details. Don't let nutrition paralysis stop you from training — the best diet is the one you actually follow.
